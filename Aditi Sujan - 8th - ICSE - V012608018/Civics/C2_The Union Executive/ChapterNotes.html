THE UNION EXECUTIVE

1. Overview of the Union Executive

The Union Executive is one of the most important organs of the Indian government. Its primary responsibility is to enforce the laws passed by the Parliament and to run the country's administration smoothly.

The Union Executive consists of:

  • The President
  • The Vice President
  • The Prime Minister
  • The Council of Ministers (to aid and advise the President)
Concept

Political vs. Permanent Executives: The President, Vice President, Prime Minister, and Council of Ministers form the political executives (holding office for a specific term). The civil servants (like IAS, IPS) are the permanent executives who act as guides to the political executives and carry out day-to-day administration.

2. The President of India

The President is the constitutional head of the Republic of India and its first citizen. The President executes the powers of the Union Government, either directly or through subordinates.

Qualifications

  • Must be a citizen of India.
  • Must be 35 years of age or above.
  • Must qualify the conditions required to be elected as a member of the Lok Sabha.
  • Must not hold any office of profit under any government or public authority.
  • Must not be a member of the parliament or any state legislature.

Presidential Election and Term

The President is elected indirectly by an Electoral College. It comprises the elected members of the Lok Sabha, Rajya Sabha, and Legislative Assemblies of the states and the Union Territories of Delhi and Puducherry.

  • Term: Elected for a term of 5 years (can be re-elected).
  • Resignation/Removal: Can resign by writing to the Vice President, or be removed through the process of impeachment for violating the Constitution.
Fact

Dr. Rajendra Prasad was the first President of India (1950-1962). The official residence of the President is the Rashtrapati Bhavan in New Delhi.

Powers of the President

The powers of the President are categorized into several distinct areas:

  • Executive Powers: Administrative head of the country. Appoints the Prime Minister, Council of Ministers, Attorney General, CAG, Election Commissioners, UPSC Chairman, State Governors, and Supreme/High Court judges.
  • Legislative Powers: Summons or prorogues the Parliament, dissolves the Lok Sabha, and addresses the Parliament. Nominates 12 members to the Rajya Sabha. A bill passed by the Parliament becomes a law only after the President's signature.
  • Financial Powers: A Money Bill can only be introduced with the President's recommendation. Controls the Contingency Fund of India to meet unforeseen expenses.
  • Judicial Powers: Appoints the Chief Justice. Has the power to grant pardons against punishments (Article 72). Enjoys judicial immunity during the term.
  • Diplomatic & Military Powers: All international treaties are concluded in the President's name. Serves as the Supreme Commander of the Armed Forces and appoints the chiefs of the Army, Navy, and Air Force. Can declare war and conclude peace.
Important

Emergency Powers: The President can declare three types of emergencies: National Emergency (Article 352) due to war/rebellion, State Emergency / President's Rule (Article 356 & 365) if a state fails to run constitutionally, and Financial Emergency (Article 360) for financial instability.

Limitations

The President is the head of the State, not the head of the government. The President acts primarily on the advice of the Council of Ministers and the Prime Minister.

3. The Vice President of India

The Vice President acts as the ex-officio Chairman of the Rajya Sabha.

  • Qualifications: Must be a citizen of India, 35+ years of age, qualified to be a Rajya Sabha member, and hold no office of profit.
  • Election & Term: Elected by an electoral college of both Houses of Parliament for a 5-year term.
  • Powers & Functions: Presides over Rajya Sabha sessions. In the absence of the President (due to death, resignation, etc.), takes over as the Acting President and enjoys all presidential powers and immunities.

4. The Prime Minister of India

The Prime Minister is the head of the government and the country. Appointed by the President, the PM is usually the leader of the majority party in the Lok Sabha.

Powers and Functions of the Prime Minister

  • Head of Council of Ministers: Forms the Council of Ministers, distributes portfolios (departments), and presides over Cabinet meetings. Can demand a minister's resignation.
  • Link to the President: Serves as the primary means of communication between the President and the Council of Ministers (Article 74 & 75).
  • Leader of the Parliament: Chief spokesperson in the Lok Sabha. Recommends the summoning, proroguing, or dissolution of the Parliament to the President.
  • Role in Foreign Affairs: Shapes the foreign policy, represents India at international forums (like the UN, SAARC), and visits foreign countries to improve relations.

5. The Council of Ministers

The Council of Ministers works under the leadership of the Prime Minister. Its core principle is collective responsibility to the Lok Sabha. If a vote of no-confidence is passed against one minister, it is treated as a vote against the entire Council, demonstrating cabinet solidarity.
