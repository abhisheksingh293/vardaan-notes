Chapter 12: Artificial Intelligence Domains

Concept

Artificial Intelligence (AI) can be defined as the ability of computer systems (hardware and software) to do tasks that normally require human beings to use intelligence, such as thinking, perceiving, learning, problem-solving, and decision-making.

1. Fields Related to Artificial Intelligence

There are several advanced fields that work together to make Artificial Intelligence possible:

Machine Learning (ML)

Machine Learning is the technique that gives computers the potential to learn from data without being explicitly programmed for every single action. It is actively used in our daily lives.

Fact

Examples of Machine Learning in Daily Life:
• Google Maps: Analyzes user data to find the fastest routes and estimate traffic jams.
• Cab Apps (Ola/Uber): ML estimates the price of a ride and the shortest route.
• Gmail: Uses ML to classify emails (Primary, Social, Spam).
• Facebook: Uses ML to identify faces and suggest photo tags automatically.

Neural Networks

The neural network is a branch of AI inspired by human biology. It replicates the human brain by coding artificial "neurons" into a machine system, allowing the computer to process complex information similarly to how a human brain does.

Deep Learning

Deep Learning is a specialized type of Machine Learning that imitates the way humans gain knowledge. It includes advanced statistics and predictive modelling to solve very complex problems.

Robotics

Robotics involves building and programming physical robots so they can operate intelligently in complex, real-world scenarios based on sensory input.

2. The Three Domains of AI

The field of Artificial Intelligence is primarily divided into three main branches or "domains":

I. Data Sciences (Data)

Data is the core of almost every AI system. AI needs massive amounts of data (text, audio, video, images) to function, learn, and grow. Data Science collects this data, maintains it in accurate datasets, and processes it to give correct outputs. Examples include personalized website recommendations and price comparison sites.

II. Computer Vision

Computer Vision is how computers "see" and understand digital images and videos. The inputs for this domain are photographs and video feeds.
Examples include:

  • Face Recognition Systems: Like unlocking a smartphone using your face, or identifying faces on Snapchat and Facebook.
  • Content-Based Image Retrieval (CBIR): Searching for images using keywords on Google or Bing.

III. Natural Language Processing (NLP)

Natural Language Processing is the ability of a computer to understand human language (whether spoken or written) and process it to give a meaningful output. Examples include smart assistants like Apple's Siri and Amazon's Alexa.

Important

Two Main Components of NLP:
1. Natural Language Understanding (NLU): Interprets the words and analyzes what the user is trying to say.
2. Natural Language Generation (NLG): Generates meaningful phrases and sentences in human language to reply to the user.

The processes involved in NLP are Text Planning (extracting relevant data), Sentence Planning (linking words into phrases), and Text Realization (combining phrases into full sentences).

3. Artificial Intelligence in Gaming

AI is heavily used in modern games. Let's look at four examples that utilize different AI domains:

  • Game 1: Rock, Paper, Scissors: This game is based on the Data Science domain. The computer predicts your next move by identifying patterns in your playing history. The more you play, the more data it has, and the better it gets at beating you!
  • Game 2: Mystery Animal: This game is based on Natural Language Processing (NLP). You have 20 chances to ask the AI yes/no questions (like "Do you eat meat?") to guess the animal. The AI uses NLP to interpret your spoken questions and answer correctly.
  • Game 3: Emoji Scavenger Hunt: This game is based on Computer Vision. The AI shows you an emoji (like a shoe), and you must point your phone camera at a real shoe. Neural networks identify if the object in the camera matches the emoji.
  • Game 4: Thing Translator: A web app that lets you point your camera at any object to hear how to say it in a different language. It combines Computer Vision (to identify the object) and NLP (to translate it).
