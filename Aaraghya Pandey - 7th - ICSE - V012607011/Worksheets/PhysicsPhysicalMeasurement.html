Especially designed for: Aaraghya Class: 7 (ICSE) Subject: Physics
Section A: Multiple Choice Questions (Conceptual)
1.
Which of the following is the correct SI unit of density?
(a) $\mathrm{g/cm^3}$    (b) $\mathrm{kg/m^3}$    (c) $\mathrm{kg/cm^3}$    (d) $\mathrm{g/m^3}$
2.
$1 \text{ hectare}$ of land is equivalent to an area of:
(a) $100 \mathrm{\ m^2}$    (b) $1,000 \mathrm{\ m^2}$    (c) $10,000 \mathrm{\ m^2}$    (d) $100,000 \mathrm{\ m^2}$
3.
The density of pure water is considered to be maximum exactly at a temperature of:
(a) $0^\circ\mathrm{C}$    (b) $100^\circ\mathrm{C}$    (c) $4^\circ\mathrm{C}$    (d) $10^\circ\mathrm{C}$
4.
The standard unit for measuring relative density is:
(a) $\mathrm{kg/m^3}$    (b) $\mathrm{g/cm^3}$    (c) $\mathrm{kg/litre}$    (d) It has no unit
5.
A density of $1 \mathrm{\ g/cm^3}$ is equal to:
(a) $10 \mathrm{\ kg/m^3}$    (b) $100 \mathrm{\ kg/m^3}$    (c) $1,000 \mathrm{\ kg/m^3}$    (d) $10,000 \mathrm{\ kg/m^3}$
6.
The instrument located on the dashboard of a vehicle that indicates its instantaneous speed is the:
(a) Odometer    (b) Speedometer    (c) Hydrometer    (d) Barometer
7.
The surface area of an irregular flat lamina (like a leaf) is most accurately estimated using a:
(a) Measuring cylinder    (b) Vernier caliper    (c) Graph paper    (d) Centimeter ruler
8.
The exact mass of an object in a laboratory is conventionally measured using a:
(a) Spring balance    (b) Measuring tape    (c) Physical (Beam) balance    (d) Measuring cylinder
9.
A volume of $1 \mathrm{\ m^3}$ is exactly equal to a liquid capacity of:
(a) $10 \text{ litres}$    (b) $100 \text{ litres}$    (c) $1,000 \text{ litres}$    (d) $10,000 \text{ litres}$
10.
The weight of a body:
(a) Remains constant everywhere in the universe    (b) Changes from place to place depending on gravity    (c) Is considered a scalar quantity    (d) Is properly measured in kilograms

Section E: Rigorous Numerical Problems (Show calculation steps in your notebook)
41.
A solid rectangular block of timber has measurable dimensions of $20 \text{ cm} \times 10 \text{ cm} \times 5 \text{ cm}$. If a physical balance determines its mass to be exactly $600 \text{ g}$, calculate its density.
42.
The mass of an empty laboratory density bottle is $25 \text{ g}$. When completely filled to the brim with distilled water, its mass becomes $75 \text{ g}$. When emptied, dried, and filled completely with an unknown organic oil, its mass is recorded as $65 \text{ g}$. Calculate the relative density of the oil.
43.
An express train smoothly travels a total distance of $360 \text{ km}$ over a period of exactly $5 \text{ hours}$. Calculate its average speed, and express the final answer in $\mathrm{m/s}$.
44.
The documented density of pure aluminium is given as $2700 \mathrm{\ kg/m^3}$ in SI units. Mathematically convert and express this density in the CGS unit of $\mathrm{g/cm^3}$.
45.
A solid crafted piece of brass, weighing exactly $170 \text{ g}$ on a beam balance, is submerged in a measuring cylinder and found to displace $20 \mathrm{\ cm^3}$ of water. Deduce its density in $\mathrm{g/cm^3}$.
46.
A modern car moving with a strictly constant speed of $54 \text{ km/h}$ takes exactly $20 \text{ seconds}$ to completely travel from one end of a suspension bridge to the other. Find the total length of the bridge in meters.
47.
A laboratory measuring cylinder initially contains $50 \text{ ml}$ of water. When an irregular metallic stone of mass $78 \text{ g}$ is carefully completely immersed in the water by a string, the water level noticeably rises to the $60 \text{ ml}$ mark. Calculate the true density of the metallic stone in $\mathrm{g/cm^3}$.
48.
A large industrial metal sheet has a calculated surface area of $5 \mathrm{\ m^2}$. Convert this area systematically into $\mathrm{cm^2}$.
49.
The relative density of liquid mercury at room temperature is exceptionally high at $13.6$. What is the absolute density of this mercury expressed formally in standard SI units?
50.
Calculate the total linear distance traveled by a commercial aeroplane flying at a constant cruising speed of $800 \text{ km/h}$ over a time duration of exactly $45 \text{ minutes}$.
Answers for Numerical Problems (Section E)
Ans 41. Density = $0.6 \mathrm{\ g/cm^3}$
Ans 42. Relative density of oil = $0.8$
Ans 43. Average Speed = $20 \mathrm{\ m/s}$
Ans 44. Density in CGS = $2.7 \mathrm{\ g/cm^3}$
Ans 45. Density of brass = $8.5 \mathrm{\ g/cm^3}$
Ans 46. Length of the bridge = $300 \mathrm{\ m}$
Ans 47. Density of the stone = $7.8 \mathrm{\ g/cm^3}$
Ans 48. Area = $50,000 \mathrm{\ cm^2}$
Ans 49. Density of mercury in SI = $13,600 \mathrm{\ kg/m^3}$
Ans 50. Distance traveled = $600 \mathrm{\ km}$
