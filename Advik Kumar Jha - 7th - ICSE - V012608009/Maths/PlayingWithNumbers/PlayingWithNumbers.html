Challenger Worksheet - Playing with Numbers
Especially designed for: Advik Class: 8 (ICSE) Subject: Mathematics
Section A: Generalized Forms & Digit Puzzles (HOTS)
1.
A two-digit number is such that the product of its digits is $14$. If $45$ is added to the number, the digits interchange their places. Find the original number.
2.
The sum of a two-digit number and the number obtained by reversing its digits is $121$. Find the number if its digits differ by $3$.
3.
A two-digit number is $4$ times the sum of its digits and twice the product of its digits. Determine the number.
4.
The sum of the digits of a three-digit number is $11$. If the digits are reversed, the new number decreases by $495$. Find the original number if the hundreds digit is $3$ times the units digit.
5.
If you subtract a two-digit number from the number formed by reversing its digits, the result is always divisible by a specific integer. Prove algebraically that this integer is $9$.
6.
Let $abc$ be a three-digit number. Show algebraically that $(abc + bca + cab)$ is always a multiple of $37$.
7.
The tens digit of a two-digit number is three times its units digit. If the sum of this number and the number formed by reversing its digits is $88$, find the number.
8.
A three-digit number $4A3$ is added to another three-digit number $984$ to give a four-digit number $13B7$, which is divisible by $11$. Find the values of $A$ and $B$.
9.
In a three-digit number, the unit's digit is twice the ten's digit and the hundred's digit is thrice the ten's digit. If the sum of its digits is $18$, what is the number?
10.
Write the generalized form of a four-digit number $pqrs$ and prove that the difference between $pqrs$ and $srqp$ is divisible by $9$.
11.
A two digit number exceeds the sum of the squares of its digits by $11$ and exceeds twice the product of its digits by $5$. Find the two digit number.
12.
The sum of the digits of a 2-digit number is $9$. If $27$ is subtracted from the number, its digits are reversed. Find the number.
13.
A three digit number is equal to $17$ times the sum of its digits. If $198$ is added to the number, the digits are reversed. The sum of the extreme digits is $1$ less than the middle digit. Find the number.
14.
Formulate the largest possible $4$-digit number using the digits $x$, $y$, $z$, $w$ (all distinct and non-zero) such that the number is divisible by $5$ but not by $2$, and $x + y = 15$.
15.
Let the number $N = 100a + 10b + c$. If $N$ is divisible by $7$, prove that $2a + 3b + c$ is also divisible by $7$.
Section B: Advanced Divisibility Tests
16.
Find the smallest and the largest value of the digit $x$ such that the number $5x423x$ is divisible by $4$.
17.
Determine the missing digit $y$ so that the number $34y56$ is completely divisible by $88$.
18.
Find the values of $a$ and $b$ if the number $6a3b72$ is divisible by both $9$ and $11$.
19.
If the number $42x7y$ is a multiple of $72$, find the value of $x + y$.
20.
Check the divisibility of $274536$ by $7$, $11$, and $13$ using the combined divisibility rule of $1001$. Show your working.
21.
What is the smallest positive integer that must be added to $54321$ to make it exactly divisible by $99$?
22.
If $N = 34567x2y$ is divisible by $45$, find all possible pairs of $(x, y)$.
23.
Find the least value of $k$ for which the number $8k432$ is a multiple of $11$.
24.
Without actual division, show that $10^{12} - 1$ is divisible by $9, 11$, and $13$.
25.
A number $712A4B$ is divisible by $36$. Find the maximum possible value of the product $A \times B$.
26.
Prove that any $6$-digit number formed by repeating a $3$-digit number (e.g., $456456$) is always divisible by $7, 11,$ and $13$.
27.
Find the smallest digit $p$ such that $123p456$ is divisible by $7$.
28.
Is the number $9^{100} - 1$ divisible by $8$? Give logical reasoning for your answer.
29.
Find the value of $x$ for which $7x86x4$ is divisible by $11$.
30.
The number $875A$ is a multiple of $6$. What are the possible values of the digit $A$?
31.
If $5A + B3 = 65$, and $A$ and $B$ are distinct non-zero digits, determine whether the number $AB$ is divisible by $3$.
32.
Demonstrate the divisibility test of $13$ on the number $16198$. Show each step clearly.
33.
Determine if $12456789$ is divisible by $8$. Provide the rule used.
34.
Find the unknown digits $x$ and $y$ such that the number $5x7y$ is divisible by $33$.
35.
What is the smallest number which when divided by $4, 5,$ and $6$ leaves a remainder of $3$ in each case?
Section C: Cryptarithms & Number Alphametics
36.
Solve the cryptarithm: $AB \times 6 = BBB$. Find the values of $A$ and $B$.
37.
Find the digits $A$, $B$, and $C$ if $1A \times A = 9A$.
38.
Solve for $A$ and $B$: $A1 + 1B = B0$.
39.
Determine the values of $P$ and $Q$ if $PQ \times P3 = 57Q$.
40.
Find the unique digits for letters $M, A, T, H$ such that $MATH \times 4 = HTAM$.
41.
Solve the addition puzzle: $SEND + MORE = MONEY$. (Find the value of each distinct letter).
42.
Find $A, B$, and $C$ in the multiplication: $AB \times C = 175$ (where $A, B, C$ are distinct digits and $C < 9$).
43.
If $2AB + AB1 = B18$, find the numerical values of $A$ and $B$.
44.
Find the value of $X$ and $Y$ in the subtraction: $XX0 - 7Y = YYY$.
45.
Solve: $A + A + A = BA$. Find the digits $A$ and $B$.
46.
Determine $A$ and $B$ if $AA + BB + CC = ABC$ (where $A, B, C$ are non-zero distinct digits).
47.
Find $P, Q, R$ such that $PQ \times R = RRR$.
48.
In the equation $CROSS + ROADS = DANGER$, given that $C = 9, R = 6$, and $S = 3$, find the remaining digits.
49.
Solve the cryptarithm $EVE \times A = VAA$.
50.
Find the values of $x$ and $y$ if $xy \times yx = 1008$.
Section D: Logical Reasoning & Magic Squares
51.
Construct a $3 \times 3$ magic square using numbers from $5$ to $13$. What is the magic constant?
52.
In a $3 \times 3$ magic square filled with integers, the sum of the elements of any row, column, or diagonal is $27$. Find the value of the central cell.
53.
I am a $4$-digit number. My thousands digit is a prime number. My hundreds digit is a perfect square. My tens digit is half my thousands digit. My units digit is $0$. I am divisible by $3$ and $4$. Who am I?
54.
Using the digits $1, 2, 3, 4, 5$ exactly once, what is the largest $5$-digit prime number you can form? Explain your reasoning.
55.
How many positive integers less than $100$ have the property that the sum of their digits is equal to the product of their digits?
56.
Let $S$ be the sum of all $3$-digit numbers that can be formed using the digits $3, 5,$ and $8$ without repetition. Find the value of $S$ and show that it is divisible by $111$.
57.
There are dogs and chickens in a farm. If you count heads, there are $45$. If you count legs, there are $130$. Use algebraic number logic to find the number of dogs.
58.
If today is Friday, what day of the week will it be after $10^{15}$ days? (Hint: Use properties of remainders and divisibility by $7$).
59.
Is it possible to distribute $50$ apples into $5$ baskets such that each basket contains an odd number of apples? Prove your answer.
60.
A boy wrote all the numbers from $1$ to $1000$. How many times did he write the digit '7' in total? Use systematic counting to justify your answer.
