Hardware Components of a Computer

Concept

A computer system consists of two primary components: Hardware and Software. Hardware refers to the physical parts of a computer that can be seen and touched, including all input, output, and processing devices. Software comprises the programs that instruct the computer on what tasks to perform.

1. External Hardware Components

External hardware components are peripherals attached to a computer externally. Some key external components include:

Monitor

Also known as a Visual Display Unit (VDU), the monitor displays the result of a process in the form of text, images, or videos. The information displayed is referred to as a soft copy. Touchscreen monitors act as both input and output devices.

Keyboard & Mouse

  • Keyboard: Used to input letters, numbers, and symbols. Modern advancements include Laser Bluetooth keyboards that detect typing on optical surfaces.
  • Mouse: Controls the cursor movement and is used to select items on the screen. It usually contains a left button, a right button, and a scroll wheel.

Printers

A printer produces a hard copy by printing text or images on paper. Types of printers include:

  • Impact Printers: Use a print head to strike an ink ribbon against paper (e.g., Dot-matrix printer).
  • Non-impact Printers: Do not use striking mechanisms, making them quieter (e.g., Inkjet and Laser printers).
Fact

3D Printers represent the latest advancement in printing technology. They create 3D physical objects from digital designs by building them layer by layer using materials like plastic, metal, or concrete.

Scanners

Scanners digitize documents and images. Common types are:

  • Flatbed Scanner: Has a flat glass surface to place the document.
  • Barcode Scanner: Scans a group of vertical lines (barcodes) to retrieve product information.

Other External Devices

  • USB Flash Drive: Also known as a pen drive, it is a portable storage device with capacities ranging from 4 GB to 1 TB.
  • Modem (Modulator/Demodulator): Converts digital information into analog and vice versa to enable data transmission over networks.
  • Ports: Connection points between a computer and external/internal devices.

2. Internal Hardware Components

Internal components are located inside the computer chassis or cabinet.

Motherboard

The motherboard is the main circuit board. It acts as the central hub where all other internal components (like integrated circuits) are connected.

Processor (CPU)

The Central Processing Unit (CPU) is a small chip that executes instructions. It consists of three main parts:

  • Arithmetic Logic Unit (ALU): Performs all arithmetic (+, -) and logical (<, >, =) operations.
  • Control Unit (CU): Manages and controls the activities of all other computer units.
  • Memory/Storage Unit (MU): Temporarily stores data and instructions required for processing.

Disk Drives

Disk drives physically store data. Types include:

  • Hard Disk Drive (HDD): Permanently fixed storage that holds massive amounts of data (up to 3 TB or more).
  • Solid-State Drive (SSD): A newer, non-volatile storage using flash memory. SSDs are more reliable, efficient, and consume less power than HDDs.
  • Compact Disc (CD) / DVD: Optical storage; a CD stores up to 700 MB, while a DVD can hold up to 4.7 GB.

Internal Cards & Power Supply

  • Sound Card: Enables the computer to produce and process audio.
  • Graphics Card: Controls the display of pictures and videos (also known as a graphics adapter).
  • SMPS (Switched-Mode Power Supply): Provides power and stabilizes voltage for the computer components.
Important

External vs. Internal Distinction: Remember that external hardware is connected via cables/ports and can be easily detached and carried around. Internal hardware is connected directly to the motherboard and is securely housed inside the computer cabinet.
