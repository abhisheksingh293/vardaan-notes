Percentage, Profit & Loss
Especially designed for: Ridhyam Class: 8 Subject: Mathematics (ICSE)
Section A: Fundamentals of Percentage
1.
Convert the fraction $\frac{3}{5}$ into a percentage.
2.
Express $0.875$ as a percentage.
3.
Convert $12\frac{1}{2}\%$ into a fraction in its simplest form.
4.
Express $45\%$ as a decimal.
5.
Find the value of $15\%$ of ₹ $600$.
6.
Evaluate: $33\frac{1}{3}\%$ of $90$ kg.
7.
What percentage of $50$ is $15$?
8.
What percentage of $2$ liters is $250$ ml?
9.
Find the number whose $12\%$ is $72$.
10.
If $16\frac{2}{3}\%$ of a number is $25$, find the number.
11.
A student scored $450$ marks out of $600$. Calculate his percentage.
12.
In a class of $40$ students, $15\%$ are absent. Find the number of students present.
13.
Convert the ratio $3:4$ to a percentage.
14.
Out of an income of ₹ $15,000$, a man spends ₹ $10,500$. What percentage of his income does he save?
15.
Find the value of $x$ if $x\%$ of $250$ is $50$.
Section B: Percentage Increase and Decrease
16.
Increase $80$ by $20\%$.
17.
Decrease $150$ by $10\%$.
18.
The price of a book increases from ₹ $200$ to ₹ $250$. Find the percentage increase.
19.
A car originally cost ₹ $4,00,000$. Its price depreciated to ₹ $3,60,000$ in a year. What is the percentage decrease?
20.
The population of a town increased by $5\%$ to reach $21,000$. Find the original population.
21.
After deducting $15\%$ from a bill, ₹ $1,700$ is left to be paid. What was the original bill amount?
22.
Ravi's salary is $25\%$ more than Raju's salary. By what percentage is Raju's salary less than Ravi's salary?
23.
The price of sugar goes up by $20\%$. By how much percentage must a housewife reduce her consumption so that the expenditure remains the same?
24.
A number is first increased by $10\%$ and then decreased by $10\%$. Find the net percentage change.
25.
If the length of a rectangle is increased by $20\%$ and its breadth is decreased by $10\%$, find the percentage change in its area.
Section C: Basic Profit and Loss
26.
Find the Profit or Loss when Cost Price (CP) = ₹ $450$ and Selling Price (SP) = ₹ $500$.
27.
Find the Profit or Loss when Cost Price (CP) = ₹ $720$ and Selling Price (SP) = ₹ $630$.
28.
Calculate the Profit Percentage if a pen bought for ₹ $20$ is sold for ₹ $25$.
29.
Calculate the Loss Percentage if a chair bought for ₹ $800$ is sold for ₹ $680$.
30.
Find the Selling Price if CP = ₹ $1,200$ and Profit = $15\%$.
31.
Find the Selling Price if CP = ₹ $3,500$ and Loss = $8\%$.
32.
Find the Cost Price if SP = ₹ $1,320$ and Profit = $10\%$.
33.
Find the Cost Price if SP = ₹ $920$ and Loss = $8\%$.
34.
A man buys a cycle for ₹ $1,400$ and sells it at a loss of $15\%$. What is the selling price?
35.
By selling a table for ₹ $3,220$, a shopkeeper gains $15\%$. Find its cost price.
36.
By selling an article for ₹ $100$, a man gains ₹ $15$. Find his gain percentage.
37.
Rohan purchased a bike for ₹ $45,000$ and spent ₹ $5,000$ on its repairs. He then sold it for ₹ $55,000$. Find his profit percentage.
38.
A shopkeeper bought $120$ apples at ₹ $5$ each. $20$ apples were rotten. He sold the remaining at ₹ $7$ each. Find his profit or loss percentage.
39.
If the cost price of $15$ articles is equal to the selling price of $12$ articles, find the profit percentage.
40.
If the selling price of $10$ items is equal to the cost price of $11$ items, what is the profit or loss percent?
Section D: Advanced Profit and Loss Applications
41.
A dealer sold two TVs for ₹ $10,000$ each. On one, he gained $20\%$, and on the other, he lost $20\%$. Find his net profit or loss percentage.
42.
A vendor buys lemons at $5$ for ₹ $10$ and sells them at $4$ for ₹ $10$. Find his gain percentage.
43.
By selling $33$ meters of cloth, a merchant gains the selling price of $11$ meters. Find his gain percentage.
44.
A man sells an article at a profit of $25\%$. If he had bought it at $20\%$ less and sold it for ₹ $10.50$ less, he would have gained $30\%$. Find the cost price.
45.
The profit earned by selling an article for ₹ $900$ is double the loss incurred when the same article is sold for ₹ $450$. At what price should it be sold to make $25\%$ profit?
46.
A dishonest shopkeeper professes to sell his goods at cost price, but uses a false weight of $900$ grams for a kilogram. Find his gain percentage.
47.
Rakesh bought an almirah for ₹ $6,250$ and spent ₹ $375$ on its transportation. He sold it at a profit of $8\%$. Find the selling price.
48.
An article is sold at a loss of $10\%$. Had it been sold for ₹ $90$ more, there would have been a gain of $5\%$. Find the original cost price.
49.
A person bought a certain number of eggs at $3$ for ₹ $5$ and an equal number at $4$ for ₹ $5$. He mixed them and sold them at $5$ for ₹ $8$. Find his gain or loss percent.
50.
A tradesman marks his goods $20\%$ above the cost price and allows a discount of $10\%$. What is his gain percent?
Section E: Marked Price and Discount
51.
Find the Marked Price (MP) if Selling Price = ₹ $450$ and Discount = $10\%$.
52.
Find the Selling Price if Marked Price = ₹ $800$ and Discount = $15\%$.
53.
A shirt is marked at ₹ $1,200$ and is sold for ₹ $960$. Find the rate of discount.
54.
Find the single discount equivalent to two successive discounts of $20\%$ and $10\%$.
55.
A shopkeeper allows a discount of $20\%$ on the marked price of an article and still makes a profit of $25\%$. If the article costs him ₹ $400$, find its marked price.
56.
By selling an umbrella for ₹ $300$, a shopkeeper gains $20\%$. During a clearance sale, he offers a $10\%$ discount on the marked price. His gain percentage during the sale is?
57.
An item marked at ₹ $840$ is sold for ₹ $714$. What is the discount and discount percentage?
58.
A fan is listed at ₹ $1500$ and a discount of $20\%$ is offered. What additional discount must be given to bring the net price to ₹ $1104$?
59.
The marked price of a watch is ₹ $1600$. After two successive discounts, it is sold for ₹ $1224$. If the first discount is $10\%$, what is the rate of the second discount?
60.
How much percent above the cost price should a shopkeeper mark his goods so that after allowing a discount of $25\%$ on the marked price, he gains $20\%$?
