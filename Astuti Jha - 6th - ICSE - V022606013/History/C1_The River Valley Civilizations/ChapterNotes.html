THE RIVER VALLEY CIVILIZATIONS

1. The Concept of Civilization

A civilization is described as an advanced culture compared to primitive cultures. It is a progressive phenomenon leading from a simple lifestyle to a complex, urbanized society.

Concept

Civilization is the stage of human social development characterized by urban development, social stratification, symbolic communication (like writing systems), and a structured political and economic setup. The hallmark of civilization is the specialization of labour.

Background and Origins

The initial development of agriculture and the domestication of farm animals began in the Neolithic period (later part of the Stone Age: 8000 BC - 4000 BC).

Important Dates

The earliest human civilizations came into existence around 3500 BC. These societies witnessed massive developments in the fields of writing, arts, science, and the establishment of economic institutions.

2. Features that led to the Development of Civilizations

The transition from a nomadic, primitive lifestyle to advanced, settled civilizations happened due to several contributing factors:

  • Favourable Climatic Conditions: Suitable weather allowed humans to settle in one place.
  • Inventive Skills: Humans used their intelligence to invent the wheel, tools, and better farming techniques.
  • Surplus Food Production: The development of agriculture meant more food than was immediately needed, allowing time for other activities.
  • Great Milestones: Inventions of writing, the technique of smelting metals, and the development of astronomy.
  • Communication: People began to interchange their ideas through language (both written and spoken).
  • Social and Political Structures: Civilizations developed social divisions and centralized power centres. Religion also began to play a dominant role in people's lives.

3. Reasons for Settlement Near Rivers

Early civilizations are often called "River Valley Civilizations" because all major early settlements grew on the banks of mighty rivers. Rivers functioned as the lifelines and unstoppable vehicles for development.

Fact

Rivers flooded every year, bringing fresh silt. This kept the fertility of the soil completely intact, allowing continuous surplus agricultural production without modern fertilizers.

  • Continuous Flow of Water: Essential for both daily survival and large-scale agriculture.
  • Fertile Land: The soil near the rivers was highly productive, supporting dense settlements.
  • Transport and Communication: Rivers acted as natural highways, making the movement of goods and people easy and cheap.

4. Major River Valley Civilizations

The river valley can be termed the "mother of civilization." The four major early civilizations of the ancient world are:

  • The Mesopotamian Civilization: Flourished on the banks of the Tigris and Euphrates rivers.
  • The Egyptian Civilization: Developed on the banks of the Nile river. (The Greek historian Herodotus famously called Egypt "The gift of the Nile").
  • The Indus Valley Civilization: Grew up on the banks of the Indus river in the Indian subcontinent.
  • The Chinese Civilization: Flourished on the banks of the Hwang Ho (Yellow River).
