Section A: Fundamental Theorem of Arithmetic & Basics (1-2 Marks)
1.

2.

3.
Given that $\text{HCF} (306, 657) = 9$, find $\text{LCM} (306, 657)$.
4.
Check whether $6^n$ can end with the digit $0$ for any natural number $n$. Give reasons for your answer.
5.

6.
Can two numbers have 18 as their HCF and 380 as their LCM? Give reasons.
7.
The HCF of two numbers is 145 and their LCM is 2175. If one number is 725, find the other number.
8.

10.

11.

Section C: Word Problems - LCM & HCF Applications (3-4 Marks)
21.
There is a circular path around a sports field. Sonia takes 18 minutes to drive one round of the field, while Ravi takes 12 minutes for the same. Suppose they both start at the same point and at the same time, and go in the same direction. After how many minutes will they meet again at the starting point?
22.
An army contingent of 616 members is to march behind an army band of 32 members in a parade. The two groups are to march in the same number of columns. What is the maximum number of columns in which they can march?
23.
A sweet seller has 420 kaju barfis and 130 badam barfis. She wants to stack them in such a way that each stack has the same number, and they take up the least area of the tray. What is the maximum number of barfis that can be placed in each stack for this purpose?
24.
Three alarm clocks ring at intervals of 4, 12, and 20 minutes respectively. If they start ringing together, after how much time will they next ring together?
25.
Two tankers contain 850 litres and 680 litres of kerosene oil respectively. Find the maximum capacity of a container which can measure the kerosene oil of both the tankers when used an exact number of times.
26.
Six bells commence tolling together and toll at intervals of 2, 4, 6, 8, 10 and 12 seconds respectively. In 30 minutes, how many times do they toll together?
27.
In a morning walk, three persons step off together. Their steps measure 80 cm, 85 cm and 90 cm respectively. What is the minimum distance each should walk so that all can cover the same distance in complete steps?
28.
The length, breadth and height of a room are 8m 25cm, 6m 75cm and 4m 50cm respectively. Find the length of the longest rod that can measure the three dimensions of the room exactly.
29.
144 cartons of Coke cans and 90 cartons of Pepsi cans are to be stacked in a canteen. If each stack is of the same height and is to contain cartons of the same drink, what would be the greatest number of cartons each stack would have?
30.
Amita, Suneha and Raghav start preparing cards for all persons of an old age home. In order to complete one card, they take 10, 16 and 20 minutes respectively. If all of them started together, after what time will they start preparing a new card together?

Section E: Answer Key & Hints
Ans 1: $3825 = 3^2 \times 5^2 \times 17$.
Ans 2: HCF = 3, LCM = 420.
Ans 3: 22338. Hint: $\text{LCM} = \frac{306 \times 657}{9}$.
Ans 4: No. Hint: Prime factorization of $6^n$ is $(2 \times 3)^n$. It does not contain the prime factor 5, which is necessary for a number to end with 0.
Ans 5: $13 \times (7 \times 11 + 1) = 13 \times 78$. Since it has more than two factors (1, 13, 78, itself), it is composite. Same logic for the second expression taking 5 common.
Ans 6: No. Hint: HCF must always exactly divide the LCM. Here, $\frac{380}{18}$ is not an integer.
Ans 7: 435. Hint: Second Number = $\frac{\text{HCF} \times \text{LCM}}{\text{First Number}} = \frac{145 \times 2175}{725}$.
Ans 8: 2520. Hint: Find the LCM of numbers 1 to 10.
Ans 9: 2. Hint: Smallest prime = 2, Smallest composite = 4. HCF(2,4) = 2.
Ans 10: HCF = 1, LCM = $pq$.
Ans 11: HCF = $xy^2$ (lowest powers), LCM = $x^3y^3$ (highest powers).
Ans 12: HCF = 30. Hint: Numbers are $2x, 3x$. LCM of $2x, 3x$ is $6x$. $6x = 180 \implies x = 30$. HCF is $x$.
Ans 13-20: Standard textbook proofs. Assume rational, represent as $\frac{a}{b}$ (coprime), square both sides or isolate the root, and show a contradiction.
Ans 21: 36 minutes. Hint: LCM of 18 and 12.
Ans 22: 8 columns. Hint: HCF of 616 and 32.
Ans 23: 10 barfis per stack. Hint: HCF of 420 and 130.
Ans 24: 60 minutes. Hint: LCM of 4, 12, 20.
Ans 25: 170 litres. Hint: HCF of 850 and 680.
Ans 26: 16 times. Hint: LCM(2,4,6,8,10,12) = 120 seconds = 2 minutes. In 30 mins, they toll $\frac{30}{2} + 1 = 16$ times (adding 1 for the first toll together at 0th second).
Ans 27: 12240 cm (or 122m 40cm). Hint: LCM of 80, 85, 90.
Ans 28: 75 cm. Hint: Convert to cm (825, 675, 450) and find HCF.
Ans 29: 18 cartons per stack. Hint: HCF of 144 and 90.
Ans 30: 80 minutes. Hint: LCM of 10, 16, 20.
Ans 31: 16. Hint: Find HCF of $(245-5)$ and $(1029-5)$, i.e., HCF(240, 1024).
Ans 32: 17. Hint: HCF of $(398-7), (436-11), (542-15)$, i.e., HCF(391, 425, 527).
Ans 33: 151. Hint: LCM(12, 16, 24, 36) + 7 = 144 + 7 = 151.
Ans 34: 204. Hint: Notice difference $(28-8=20)$ and $(32-12=20)$ is same. Required number = $\text{LCM}(28, 32) - 20 = 224 - 20 = 204$.
Ans 35: 999720. Hint: LCM of 24, 15, 36 is 360. Greatest 6-digit is 999999. Divide 999999 by 360, remainder is 279. Subtract 279 from 999999.
Ans 36: Size = 24 inches (2 feet). Tiles required = 20. Hint: 10 ft = 120 in, 8 ft = 96 in. HCF(120, 96) = 24 in. Area = $\frac{120 \times 96}{24 \times 24} = 20$.
