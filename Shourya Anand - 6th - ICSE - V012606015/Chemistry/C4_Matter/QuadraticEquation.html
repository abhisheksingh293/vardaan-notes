Section A: Basics, Identification & Roots (1-2 Marks)
1.
Check whether the following is a quadratic equation: $(x+1)^2 = 2(x-3)$.
2.
Check whether $(x-2)(x+1) = (x-1)(x+3)$ is a quadratic equation.
3.
Determine whether $x = -\sqrt{2}$ is a solution of the quadratic equation $\sqrt{2}x^2 + 7x + 5\sqrt{2} = 0$.
4.
If $x = 3$ is a root of the quadratic equation $x^2 - 2kx - 6 = 0$, find the value of $k$.
5.
Find the value of $p$ so that $-2$ is a root of the equation $px^2 - 3x + 2 = 0$.
6.
If $1/2$ is a root of the equation $x^2 + kx - 5/4 = 0$, then find the value of $k$.
7.
Represent the following situation in the form of a quadratic equation: "The area of a rectangular plot is $528 \text{ m}^2$. The length of the plot (in metres) is one more than twice its breadth."
8.
Rohan's mother is 26 years older than him. The product of their ages (in years) 3 years from now will be 360. Formulate the quadratic equation to find Rohan's present age.
9.
Check whether $(x+2)^3 = 2x(x^2 - 1)$ is a quadratic equation.
10.
Check if $x^2 + 3x + 1 = (x-2)^2$ is a quadratic equation.
11.
Write a quadratic equation whose roots are $2 + \sqrt{3}$ and $2 - \sqrt{3}$.
12.
If $x = \frac{2}{3}$ and $x = -3$ are roots of the quadratic equation $ax^2 + 7x + b = 0$, find the values of $a$ and $b$.
13.
Find the value of $k$ if $x=2$ is a root of the equation $kx^2 + 2x - 3 = 0$.
14.
If one root of the quadratic equation $ax^2+bx+c=0$ is zero, what is the required condition for the coefficients?
15.
Can a quadratic equation have exactly three real roots? Justify your answer.

23.

Solve the quadratic equation: $4x^2 - 4a^2x + (a^4 - b^4) = 0$.

27.
Solve for $x$: $3\left(\frac{3x-1}{2x+3}\right) - 2\left(\frac{2x+3}{3x-1}\right) = 5$, where $x \neq \frac{1}{3}, \frac{-3}{2}$.
28.

29.

31.

32.
Solve the quadratic equation: $x^2 - 2ax + (a^2 - b^2) = 0$.
33.
Solve for $x$: $p^2x^2 + (p^2 - q^2)x - q^2 = 0$.
34.

Section C: Nature of Roots & Finding Unknowns (3 Marks)
36.

37.
Find the value(s) of $k$ for which the quadratic equation $2x^2 + kx + 3 = 0$ has two real and equal roots.
38.
Find the value of $k$ for which the equation $kx(x-2) + 6 = 0$ has two equal roots.
39.
For what value of $k$ does the quadratic equation $(k-12)x^2 + 2(k-12)x + 2 = 0$ have equal roots?
40.
If the equation $(1+m^2)x^2 + 2mcx + c^2 - a^2 = 0$ has equal roots, show that $c^2 = a^2(1 + m^2)$.
41.
Find the values of $p$ for which the quadratic equation $4x^2 + px + 3 = 0$ has equal roots.
42.
If $-5$ is a root of the quadratic equation $2x^2 + px - 15 = 0$ and the quadratic equation $p(x^2 + x) + k = 0$ has equal roots, find the value of $k$.
43.
Determine whether the quadratic equation $3x^2 - 5x + 2 = 0$ has real roots. If so, find them.
44.
Show that the equation $2x^2 + 5\sqrt{3}x + 16 = 0$ has no real roots.
45.
Find the value of $p$ for which the roots of the equation $px^2 - 14x + 8 = 0$ are in the ratio $3:4$.
46.
Find the value of $k$ if the equation $x^2 - 2x(1+3k) + 7(3+2k) = 0$ has real and equal roots.
47.
Find the values of $k$ for which the quadratic equation $kx^2 + 6x + 1 = 0$ has real and distinct roots.
48.
Find the values of $p$ for which the quadratic equation $px^2 - 2\sqrt{5}px + 15 = 0$ has no real roots.
49.
Prove that the roots of the equation $x^2 - 2ax - b^2 = 0$ are always real for all real values of $a$ and $b$.
50.
If the roots of the equation $(b-c)x^2 + (c-a)x + (a-b) = 0$ are equal, prove that $2b = a+c$.
Section D: Competency Word Problems - All Typologies (4-5 Marks)
51.
The sum of the squares of two consecutive odd positive integers is 290. Find the integers.
52.
A train travels $360\text{ km}$ at a uniform speed. If the speed had been $5\text{ km/h}$ more, it would have taken 1 hour less for the same journey. Find the speed of the train.
53.
A motor boat whose speed is $18\text{ km/h}$ in still water takes 1 hour more to go $24\text{ km}$ upstream than to return downstream to the same spot. Find the speed of the stream.
54.
Two water taps together can fill a tank in $9 \frac{3}{8}$ hours. The tap of larger diameter takes 10 hours less than the smaller one to fill the tank separately. Find the time in which each tap can separately fill the tank.
55.
The sum of the areas of two squares is $468\text{ m}^2$. If the difference of their perimeters is $24\text{ m}$, find the sides of the two squares.
56.
A flight is delayed by 30 minutes. To reach its destination $1500\text{ km}$ away on time, the pilot increases the speed by $250\text{ km/h}$ from its usual speed. Find the usual speed of the plane.
57.
A natural number, when increased by 12, equals 160 times its reciprocal. Find the number.
58.
The numerator of a fraction is 3 less than its denominator. If 2 is added to both the numerator and the denominator, then the sum of the new fraction and original fraction is $\frac{29}{20}$. Find the original fraction.
59.
In a class test, the sum of Shefali's marks in Mathematics and English is 30. Had she got 2 marks more in Mathematics and 3 marks less in English, the product of their marks would have been 210. Find her marks in the two subjects.
60.
A pole has to be erected at a point on the boundary of a circular park of diameter 13 metres in such a way that the differences of its distances from two diametrically opposite fixed gates A and B on the boundary is 7 metres. Is it possible to do so? If yes, at what distances from the two gates should the pole be erected?
61.
A piece of cloth costs ₹200. If the piece was 5 m longer and each metre of cloth costed ₹2 less, the cost of the piece would have remained unchanged. How long is the piece and what is its original rate per metre?
62.
Out of a group of swans, $\frac{7}{2}$ times the square root of the number are playing on the shore of a tank. The two remaining ones are playing in deep water. What is the total number of swans?
63.
Divide 29 into two parts such that the sum of their squares is 425.
64.
The sum of a number and its reciprocal is $2 \frac{1}{30}$. Find the number.
65.
A two-digit number is such that the product of its digits is 18. When 63 is subtracted from the number, the digits interchange their places. Find the number.
66.
A girl is twice as old as her sister. Four years hence, the product of their ages (in years) will be 160. Find their present ages.
67.
Person A takes 6 days less than Person B to finish a piece of work. If both work together they finish it in 4 days. Find the time taken by B alone to finish the work.
68.
An express train takes 1 hour less than a passenger train to travel $132\text{ km}$ between Mysore and Bangalore. If the average speed of the express train is $11\text{ km/h}$ more than that of the passenger train, find the average speed of the two trains.
69.
The hypotenuse of a right-angled triangle is $3\sqrt{10}\text{ cm}$. If the smaller leg is tripled and the longer leg doubled, the new hypotenuse becomes $9\sqrt{5}\text{ cm}$. How long are the legs of the triangle?
70.
A shopkeeper buys a number of books for ₹1200. If he had bought 10 more books for the same amount, each book would have cost ₹20 less. How many books did he buy initially?
71.
The numerator of a fraction is 1 less than its denominator. If 3 is added to each of the numerator and denominator, the fraction is increased by $\frac{3}{28}$. Find the original fraction.
72.
The perimeter of a right-angled triangle is $60\text{ cm}$. Its hypotenuse is $25\text{ cm}$. Find the area of the triangle.
73.
Two pipes running together can fill a cistern in $3 \frac{1}{13}$ minutes. If one pipe takes 3 minutes more than the other to fill it, find the time in which each pipe would fill the cistern separately.
74.
Some students planned a picnic. The total budget for food was ₹2000. But 5 students failed to attend the picnic and thus the cost of food for each member increased by ₹20. How many students attended the picnic and how much did each pay for the food?
75.
The difference of two natural numbers is 5 and the difference of their reciprocals is $\frac{1}{10}$. Find the numbers.
