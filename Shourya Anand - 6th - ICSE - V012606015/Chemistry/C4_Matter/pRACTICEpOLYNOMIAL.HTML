Polynomials: Board Masterclass (Target 100/100)
Student Name: ____________________________________ Class: 10 CBSE Subject: Mathematics
Section A: Basic Concepts & Graphical Representation (1 Mark)
1.
If the graph of a polynomial intersects the x-axis at exactly three distinct points, how many zeroes does the polynomial have?
2.
Find the zeroes of the quadratic polynomial $x^2 - 9$.
3.
Write a quadratic polynomial whose sum and product of zeroes are $0$ and $\sqrt{5}$ respectively.
4.
If one zero of the polynomial $p(x) = (k^2+4)x^2 + 13x + 4k$ is the reciprocal of the other, find the value of $k$.
5.
If one zero of the quadratic polynomial $x^2 + 3x + k$ is $2$, find the value of $k$.
6.
Find a quadratic polynomial whose zeroes are $-3$ and $4$.
7.
If the product of the zeroes of the polynomial $ax^2 - 6x - 6$ is $4$, find the value of $a$.
8.
What is the geometrical shape of the graph of a quadratic polynomial $ax^2 + bx + c$?
9.
If $\alpha$ and $\beta$ are zeroes of the polynomial $x^2 - 7x + 10$, find the value of $\alpha + \beta$.
10.
Find the number of zeroes of a polynomial $p(x)$ if its graph is parallel to the x-axis and does not intersect it.
Section B: Finding Zeroes & Verifying Relationships (2-3 Marks)
11.
Find the zeroes of the quadratic polynomial $4s^2 - 4s + 1$ and verify the relationship between the zeroes and the coefficients.
12.
Find the zeroes of the polynomial $t^2 - 15$ and verify the relationship between the zeroes and the coefficients.
13.
Find the zeroes of the polynomial $3x^2 - x - 4$ and verify the relationship between the zeroes and the coefficients.
14.
Find the zeroes of the polynomial $\sqrt{3}x^2 - 8x + 4\sqrt{3}$ and verify the relationship between the zeroes and its coefficients.
15.
Form a quadratic polynomial whose zeroes are $3 + \sqrt{2}$ and $3 - \sqrt{2}$.
16.
If the sum of the zeroes of the polynomial $p(x) = (k^2-14)x^2 - 2x - 12$ is $1$, find the value(s) of $k$.
17.
If one zero of the polynomial $3x^2 - 8x + 2k+1$ is seven times the other, find the value of $k$.
18.
Find a quadratic polynomial whose zeroes are the reciprocals of the zeroes of the polynomial $ax^2 + bx + c$.
19.
If $\alpha$ and $\beta$ are the zeroes of the polynomial $x^2 - 5x + c$, and $\alpha - \beta = 1$, find the value of $c$.
20.
If $\alpha$ and $\beta$ are the zeroes of the quadratic polynomial $f(x) = x^2 - px + q$, prove that $\frac{\alpha^2}{\beta^2} + \frac{\beta^2}{\alpha^2} = \frac{p^4}{q^2} - \frac{4p^2}{q} + 2$.
Section C: Advanced $\alpha, \beta$ Manipulations (3-4 Marks)
21.
If $\alpha$ and $\beta$ are the zeroes of the quadratic polynomial $f(x) = 2x^2 + 5x + k$ such that $\alpha^2 + \beta^2 + \alpha\beta = \frac{21}{4}$, find the value of $k$.
22.
If $\alpha$ and $\beta$ are the zeroes of the polynomial $f(x) = x^2 - 8x + k$ such that the sum of the squares of the zeroes is $40$, find the value of $k$.
23.
If $\alpha$ and $\beta$ are the zeroes of the polynomial $p(x) = x^2 - p(x+1) - c$, show that $(\alpha+1)(\beta+1) = 1-c$.
24.
If $\alpha$ and $\beta$ are the zeroes of the quadratic polynomial $f(x) = x^2 - 4x + 3$, form a quadratic polynomial whose zeroes are $3\alpha$ and $3\beta$.
25.
If the squared difference of the zeroes of the quadratic polynomial $f(x) = x^2 + px + 45$ is equal to $144$, find the value(s) of $p$.
26.
If $\alpha$ and $\beta$ are the zeroes of the polynomial $f(x) = ax^2 + bx + c$, find the value of $\frac{1}{\alpha} + \frac{1}{\beta} - 2\alpha\beta$ in terms of $a, b$, and $c$.
27.
Find the value of $k$ if the zeroes of the quadratic polynomial $kx^2 + 4x + 4$ are $\alpha$ and $\beta$, and they satisfy the relation $\alpha^2 + \beta^2 = 24$.
28.
If the zeroes of the polynomial $x^2 + px + q$ are double in value to the zeroes of $2x^2 - 5x - 3$, find the values of $p$ and $q$.
29.
A quadratic polynomial graph is a downward-opening parabola that intersects the x-axis at $(-2, 0)$ and $(3, 0)$. It also passes through the y-axis at $(0, 12)$. Find the expression for this polynomial.
30.
If $\alpha$ and $\beta$ are the zeroes of the quadratic polynomial $f(x) = x^2 - 1$, find a quadratic polynomial whose zeroes are $\frac{2\alpha}{\beta}$ and $\frac{2\beta}{\alpha}$.
Section D: Answer Key & Hints
Ans 1: 3 zeroes.
Ans 2: $3, -3$. Hint: $x^2 - 9 = 0 \implies (x-3)(x+3) = 0$.
Ans 3: $x^2 - \sqrt{5}$. Hint: $k[x^2 - (\text{Sum})x + \text{Product}]$.
Ans 4: $k = 2$. Hint: Product of zeroes = 1 $\implies \frac{c}{a} = 1 \implies \frac{4k}{k^2+4} = 1$.
Ans 5: $k = -10$. Hint: Substitute $x=2$, $p(2)=0$.
Ans 6: $x^2 + x - 12$. Hint: Sum = 1, Product = -12.
Ans 7: $a = -\frac{3}{2}$. Hint: $\frac{c}{a} = 4 \implies \frac{-6}{a} = 4$.
Ans 8: Parabola.
Ans 9: $7$. Hint: Sum of zeroes $\alpha + \beta = -\frac{b}{a}$.
Ans 10: 0 zeroes.
Ans 11: Zeroes are $\frac{1}{2}, \frac{1}{2}$. Sum = $1 = -\frac{-4}{4}$, Product = $\frac{1}{4} = \frac{1}{4}$.
Ans 12: Zeroes are $\sqrt{15}, -\sqrt{15}$. Sum = $0 = -\frac{0}{1}$, Product = $-15 = \frac{-15}{1}$.
Ans 13: Zeroes are $\frac{4}{3}, -1$. Sum = $\frac{1}{3} = -\frac{-1}{3}$, Product = $-\frac{4}{3} = \frac{-4}{3}$.
Ans 14: Zeroes are $2\sqrt{3}, \frac{2}{\sqrt{3}}$. Sum = $\frac{8}{\sqrt{3}} = -\frac{-8}{\sqrt{3}}$, Product = $4 = \frac{4\sqrt{3}}{\sqrt{3}}$.
Ans 15: $x^2 - 6x + 7$. Hint: Sum = $6$, Product = $(3)^2 - (\sqrt{2})^2 = 7$.
Ans 16: $k = 4, -4$. Hint: $-\frac{b}{a} = 1 \implies \frac{2}{k^2-14} = 1 \implies k^2 - 14 = 2$.
Ans 17: $k = \frac{2}{3}$. Hint: Let zeroes be $\alpha, 7\alpha$. Sum = $8\alpha = \frac{8}{3} \implies \alpha = \frac{1}{3}$. Product = $7\alpha^2 = \frac{2k+1}{3}$.
Ans 18: $cx^2 + bx + a$. Hint: New sum = $\frac{1}{\alpha} + \frac{1}{\beta} = \frac{\alpha+\beta}{\alpha\beta} = -\frac{b}{c}$. New product = $\frac{a}{c}$.
Ans 19: $c = 6$. Hint: $(\alpha-\beta)^2 = (\alpha+\beta)^2 - 4\alpha\beta \implies 1^2 = 5^2 - 4c$.
Ans 20: Proof based on expanding $\left(\frac{\alpha}{\beta} + \frac{\beta}{\alpha}\right)^2 = \left(\frac{\alpha^2+\beta^2}{\alpha\beta}\right)^2 = \left(\frac{p^2-2q}{q}\right)^2$.
Ans 21: $k = 2$. Hint: $\alpha^2 + \beta^2 + \alpha\beta = (\alpha+\beta)^2 - \alpha\beta = \frac{25}{4} - \frac{k}{2}$. Equate to $\frac{21}{4}$.
Ans 22: $k = 12$. Hint: $\alpha^2 + \beta^2 = (\alpha+\beta)^2 - 2\alpha\beta = 64 - 2k = 40$.
Ans 23: Proof. Hint: Expand $f(x) = x^2 - px - (p+c)$. $\alpha+\beta = p$, $\alpha\beta = -(p+c)$. Expand LHS: $\alpha\beta + \alpha + \beta + 1$.
Ans 24: $x^2 - 12x + 27$. Hint: $\alpha=3, \beta=1$. New zeroes are $9$ and $3$. Sum=12, Prod=27.
Ans 25: $p = 18$ or $-18$. Hint: $(\alpha-\beta)^2 = (\alpha+\beta)^2 - 4\alpha\beta = p^2 - 180 = 144$.
Ans 26: $\frac{-b - 2c^2}{ac}$. Hint: $\frac{\alpha+\beta}{\alpha\beta} - 2\alpha\beta = \frac{-b/a}{c/a} - \frac{2c}{a}$.
Ans 27: $k = -1, \frac{2}{3}$. Hint: $(\alpha+\beta)^2 - 2\alpha\beta = (-\frac{4}{k})^2 - 2(\frac{4}{k}) = \frac{16}{k^2} - \frac{8}{k} = 24$. Multiply by $k^2$ to form quadratic in $k$.
Ans 28: $p = -5, q = -6$. Hint: Zeroes of $2x^2-5x-3$ are $3, -\frac{1}{2}$. Doubled zeroes are $6, -1$. Form new poly $x^2 - 5x - 6$.
Ans 29: $p(x) = -2x^2 + 2x + 12$. Hint: Roots $-2, 3 \implies y=a(x+2)(x-3)$. At $x=0, y=12 \implies 12 = a(2)(-3) \implies a=-2$.
Ans 30: $x^2 + 4x + 4$. Hint: Zeroes of $x^2-1$ are $1, -1$. New zeroes: $\frac{2(1)}{-1} = -2$ and $\frac{2(-1)}{1} = -2$. Sum = -4, Prod = 4.
