Pair of Linear Equations: Board Masterclass (Target 100/100)
Student Name: ____________________________________ Class: 10 CBSE Subject: Mathematics
Section A: Consistency & Graphical Analysis (1-2 Marks)
1.
Without drawing the graph, find whether the lines representing the pair of linear equations $5x - 4y + 8 = 0$ and $7x + 6y - 9 = 0$ intersect at a point, are parallel, or are coincident.
2.
Find the value of $k$ for which the system of equations $x + 2y = 3$ and $5x + ky + 7 = 0$ has a unique solution.
3.
For what value of $k$, will the following pair of linear equations have infinitely many solutions? $kx + 3y = k - 3$; $12x + ky = k$.
4.
Determine the value of $c$ for which the pair of equations $cx - y = 2$ and $6x - 2y = 3$ will have no solution.
5.
Check whether the pair of equations $x + 3y = 6$ and $2x - 3y = 12$ is consistent. If so, solve them graphically.
6.
Half the perimeter of a rectangular garden, whose length is $4\text{ m}$ more than its width, is $36\text{ m}$. Formulate the linear equations for this situation and find the dimensions graphically.
7.
Given the linear equation $2x + 3y - 8 = 0$, write another linear equation in two variables such that the geometrical representation of the pair so formed is: (i) intersecting lines (ii) parallel lines (iii) coincident lines.
8.
Find whether the following pair of linear equations has a unique solution. If yes, find the solution: $7x - 4y = 49$ and $5x - 6y = 57$.
9.
Find the area of the triangle formed by the lines $x = 3$, $y = 4$ and $x = y$.
10.
Draw the graphs of the equations $x - y + 1 = 0$ and $3x + 2y - 12 = 0$. Determine the coordinates of the vertices of the triangle formed by these lines and the x-axis, and shade the triangular region.
Section B: Algebraic Methods - Substitution & Elimination (2-3 Marks)
11.
Solve the following pair of linear equations by the substitution method: $3x - 5y - 4 = 0$ and $9x = 2y + 7$.
12.
Solve using the elimination method: $\frac{x}{2} + \frac{2y}{3} = -1$ and $x - \frac{y}{3} = 3$.
13.
Solve the following system of equations: $0.2x + 0.3y = 1.3$ and $0.4x + 0.5y = 2.3$.
14.
Solve for $x$ and $y$: $\sqrt{2}x + \sqrt{3}y = 0$ and $\sqrt{3}x - \sqrt{8}y = 0$.
15.
Solve: $217x + 131y = 913$ and $131x + 217y = 827$.
16.
Solve for $x$ and $y$: $ax + by = c$ and $bx + ay = 1 + c$.
17.
Solve the equations $2x + 3y = 11$ and $2x - 4y = -24$ and hence find the value of '$m$' for which $y = mx + 3$.
18.
Find the values of $a$ and $b$ for which the following system of equations has infinitely many solutions: $2x + 3y = 7$ and $(a - b)x + (a + b)y = 3a + b - 2$.
19.
Solve: $\frac{ax}{b} - \frac{by}{a} = a + b$ and $ax - by = 2ab$.
20.
Solve the pair of equations: $152x - 378y = -74$ and $-378x + 152y = -604$.
Section C: Word Problems - Numbers, Digits & Ages (3-4 Marks)
21.
The difference between two numbers is 26 and one number is three times the other. Find them.
22.
The sum of a two-digit number and the number obtained by reversing the digits is 66. If the digits of the number differ by 2, find the number. How many such numbers are there?
23.
Seven times a given two-digit number is equal to four times the number obtained by reversing the order of the digits. If the difference of the digits is 3, determine the number.
24.
The sum of a two-digit number and the number formed by interchanging its digits is 110. If 10 is subtracted from the first number, the new number is 4 more than 5 times the sum of the digits in the first number. Find the first number.
25.
Five years hence, the age of Jacob will be three times that of his son. Five years ago, Jacob's age was seven times that of his son. What are their present ages?
26.
The present age of a father is three years more than three times the age of his son. Three years hence, father's age will be 10 years more than twice the age of the son. Determine their present ages.
27.
I am three times as old as my son. Five years later, I shall be two and a half times as old as my son. How old am I and how old is my son?
28.
Two years ago, a father was five times as old as his son. Two years later, his age will be 8 more than three times the age of the son. Find the present ages of father and son.
29.
A two-digit number is 3 more than 4 times the sum of its digits. If 18 is added to the number, the digits are reversed. Find the number.
30.
Ten years ago, a mother was 4 times as old as her daughter. Ten years hence, she will be twice as old as her daughter. Determine their present ages.
Section D: Word Problems - Fractions, Commercial, Geometry & Speed (4-5 Marks)
31.
A fraction becomes $\frac{9}{11}$, if 2 is added to both the numerator and the denominator. If 3 is added to both the numerator and the denominator it becomes $\frac{5}{6}$. Find the fraction.
32.
The sum of the numerator and denominator of a fraction is 3 less than twice the denominator. If the numerator and denominator are decreased by 1, the numerator becomes half the denominator. Determine the fraction.
33.
The taxi charges in a city consist of a fixed charge together with the charge for the distance covered. For a distance of 10 km, the charge paid is ₹105 and for a journey of 15 km, the charge paid is ₹155. What are the fixed charges and the charge per km? How much does a person have to pay for travelling a distance of 25 km?
34.
A lending library has a fixed charge for the first three days and an additional charge for each day thereafter. Saritha paid ₹27 for a book kept for seven days, while Susy paid ₹21 for the book she kept for five days. Find the fixed charge and the charge for each extra day.
35.
Meena went to a bank to withdraw ₹2000. She asked the cashier to give her ₹50 and ₹100 notes only. Meena got 25 notes in all. Find how many notes of ₹50 and ₹100 she received.
36.
Yash scored 40 marks in a test, getting 3 marks for each right answer and losing 1 mark for each wrong answer. Had 4 marks been awarded for each correct answer and 2 marks been deducted for each incorrect answer, then Yash would have scored 50 marks. How many questions were there in the test?
37.
The area of a rectangle gets reduced by 9 square units, if its length is reduced by 5 units and breadth is increased by 3 units. If we increase the length by 3 units and the breadth by 2 units, the area increases by 67 square units. Find the dimensions of the rectangle.
38.
In a $\triangle ABC$, $\angle C = 3\angle B = 2(\angle A + \angle B)$. Find the three angles.
39.
Places A and B are 100 km apart on a highway. One car starts from A and another from B at the same time. If the cars travel in the same direction at different speeds, they meet in 5 hours. If they travel towards each other, they meet in 1 hour. What are the speeds of the two cars?
40.
A train covered a certain distance at a uniform speed. If the train would have been 10 km/h faster, it would have taken 2 hours less than the scheduled time. And, if the train were slower by 10 km/h, it would have taken 3 hours more than the scheduled time. Find the distance covered by the train.
