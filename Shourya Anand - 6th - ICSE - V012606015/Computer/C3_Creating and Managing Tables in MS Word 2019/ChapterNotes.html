Creating and Managing Tables in MS Word 2019

Concept

Tables are an arrangement of data in rows and columns. They are used to organize and present data in a neat and ordered way. Data in a tabular form makes it easier to read, analyze, and compare.

  • Row: The horizontal arrangement of data is called a row.
  • Column: The vertical arrangement of data is called a column.
  • Cell: The intersection of a row and a column is called a cell.

1. Creating a Table

With the help of Microsoft Word 2019, you can insert a variety of tables into a Word document. There are two main ways to create a table:

Method I: Using the Grid

  • Click on the Insert tab.
  • Click the Table drop-down button in the Tables group.
  • Drag the mouse pointer across the grid to select the desired number of rows and columns.
  • Click on the last square of the selected range to insert the table in your document.

Note: You can use this method to insert a table of maximum 8 rows and 10 columns.

Method II: Using the Insert Table Option

  • Click on the Insert tab.
  • Click on the Table drop-down button in the Tables group.
  • From the drop-down list, click on the Insert Table... option.
  • The Insert Table dialog box appears. Enter the required number of rows and columns under Table size.
  • Click on OK. The table will be inserted.
Important

A Microsoft Word table can have any number of rows but a maximum of 63 columns.

2. Entering Data into Cells

After creating a table, you can enter data into the cells easily:

  • Click to place the cursor in the cell where the data is to be entered. Then, type the data.
  • Press the Tab key to move the cursor to the next cell on the right, or use the arrow keys to move from one cell to another in any direction.
  • Click anywhere outside the table once you have finished entering the data.

3. Modifying a Table

After entering data into a table, you can make changes to it if you need to. You can insert or delete rows/columns, change row height/column width, merge or split cells, and apply borders and shading.

3.1 Inserting a Row or Column

  • Click on the cell adjacent to where the new row or column is to be added.
  • Click on the Layout tab under the Table Tools contextual tab.
  • In the Rows & Columns group, click on any of these options:
    • Insert Above: to insert a row above the selected cell.
    • Insert Below: to insert a row below the selected cell.
    • Insert Left: to insert a column on the left of the selected cell.
    • Insert Right: to insert a column on the right of the selected cell.

3.2 Deleting a Row or Column

  • Click on any cell in the row or column which is to be deleted.
  • Click on the Layout tab under Table Tools.
  • In the Rows & Columns group, click on the Delete drop-down button.
  • Click on the desired option from the drop-down list (e.g., Delete Rows, Delete Columns).
Fact

Cool Tip: You can also use the context menu by right-clicking on a cell to access options like inserting rows/columns, deleting cells, or changing table properties!

3.3 Changing the Row Height

  • Right-click on any cell in a row whose height you want to change.
  • From the context menu, click on the Table Properties... option.
  • The Table Properties dialog box appears. Click on the Row tab.
  • Click on the Specify height check box and type the required row height.
  • Click on OK.

3.4 Changing the Column Width

  • Right-click on any cell in a column whose width you want to change.
  • From the context menu, click on the Table Properties... option.
  • The Table Properties dialog box appears. Click on the Column tab.
  • Click on the Preferred width check box and type the required column width.
  • Click on OK.

4. Merging and Splitting Cells

4.1 Merging Cells

To merge means to combine or join. You can join two or more cells into a single cell. This feature is often used to create headings that span across multiple columns.

  • Select the cells you wish to merge into a single cell.
  • Click on the Layout tab under Table Tools.
  • In the Merge group, click on Merge Cells.

4.2 Splitting Cells

Splitting cells is the opposite of merging cells. When you split a cell, you divide it into two or more distinct cells. The text of the original cell will appear in the first cell of the split cells.

  • Click on the cell you wish to split.
  • Click on the Layout tab under Table Tools.
  • In the Merge group, click on the Split Cells option.
  • In the Split Cells dialog box, enter the number of columns and rows you wish to split the cell into.
  • Click on OK.

5. Resizing a Table

  • Move the pointer over the table until the resize handle (a small square) appears on the lower-right corner.
  • Press and hold the left mouse button on this handle and drag to increase or decrease the size of the entire table.

6. Applying Borders and Shading

To enhance the appearance of a table, you can apply a variety of border and shading styles.

  • Click anywhere inside the table.
  • Click on the Design tab under Table Tools.
  • Click on the Borders drop-down button in the Borders group, and select Borders and Shading...
  • In the dialog box, under the Borders tab, you can choose the border style, color, and width.
  • To apply a background color, click on the Shading tab and select a color from the Fill drop-down menu.
  • Click on OK.

7. Alignment of Text in a Table

Alignment of data refers to the positioning of data within cells. Word has several alignment options to set the orientation of text. Click on the Layout tab in the Alignment group to choose from 9 options:

  • Align Top Left: Aligns text to the top-left of the cell.
  • Align Top Center: Centers text and aligns it to the top.
  • Align Top Right: Aligns text to the top-right.
  • Align Center Left: Centers text vertically and aligns to the left.
  • Align Center: Centers text both horizontally and vertically.
  • Align Center Right: Centers text vertically and aligns to the right.
  • Align Bottom Left: Aligns text to the bottom-left corner.
  • Align Bottom Center: Centers text horizontally and aligns to the bottom.
  • Align Bottom Right: Aligns text to the bottom-right of the cell.
