Worksheet - Direct and Inverse Proportions
Student Name: ____________________________________ Class: 8 (CBSE) Subject: Mathematics
Section A: Direct Proportion
1.
The cost of $15$ pens is ₹$150$. Find the cost of $25$ such pens.
2.
A machine in a soft drink factory fills $840$ bottles in $6$ hours. How many bottles will it fill in $5$ hours?
3.
A $5 \text{ m } 60 \text{ cm}$ high vertical pole casts a shadow $3 \text{ m } 20 \text{ cm}$ long. Find at the same time the length of the shadow cast by another pole $10 \text{ m } 50 \text{ cm}$ high.
4.
A photograph of a bacteria enlarged $50,000$ times attains a length of $5 \text{ cm}$. What is the actual length of the bacteria?
5.
In a model of a ship, the mast is $9 \text{ cm}$ high, while the mast of the actual ship is $12 \text{ m}$ high. If the length of the actual ship is $28 \text{ m}$, how long is the model ship?
6.
Suppose $2 \text{ kg}$ of sugar contains $9 \times 10^6$ crystals. How many sugar crystals are there in $5 \text{ kg}$ of sugar?
7.
Rashmi has a road map with a scale of $1 \text{ cm}$ representing $18 \text{ km}$. She drives on a road for $72 \text{ km}$. What would be her distance covered in the map?
8.
A loaded truck travels $14 \text{ km}$ in $25$ minutes. If the speed remains the same, how far can it travel in $5$ hours?
9.
If the weight of $12$ sheets of thick paper is $40$ grams, how many sheets of the same paper would weigh $2 \frac{1}{2} \text{ kilograms}$?
10.
A train is moving at a uniform speed of $75 \text{ km/h}$. How far will it travel in $20$ minutes?
11.
Continuing with the previous train moving at $75 \text{ km/h}$, find the time required to cover a distance of $250 \text{ km}$.
12.
The scale of a map is given as $1 : 30,000,000$. Two cities are $4 \text{ cm}$ apart on the map. Find the actual distance between them in kilometers.
13.
If $8 \text{ meters}$ of cloth costs ₹$250$, find the cost of $5.8 \text{ meters}$ of the same cloth.
14.
An extension cord of $15 \text{ m}$ costs ₹$450$. What is the length of a cord that costs ₹$1050$?
15.
If a typist types $540$ words in half an hour, how many words will she type in $12$ minutes?
Section B: Inverse Proportion
16.
$6$ pipes are required to fill a tank in $1 \text{ hour } 20 \text{ minutes}$. How long will it take if only $5$ pipes of the same type are used?
17.
There are $100$ students in a hostel. Food provision for them is for $20$ days. How long will these provisions last, if $25$ more students join the group?
18.
A batch of bottles was packed in $25$ boxes with $12$ bottles in each box. If the same batch is packed using $20$ bottles in each box, how many boxes would be filled?
19.
A farmer has enough food to feed $20$ animals in his cattle for $6$ days. How long would the food last if there were $10$ more animals in his cattle?
20.
A contractor estimates that $3$ persons could rewire Jasminder's house in $4$ days. If he uses $4$ persons instead of $3$, how long should they take to complete the job?
21.
A car takes $2$ hours to reach a destination by travelling at the speed of $60 \text{ km/h}$. How long will it take when the car travels at the speed of $80 \text{ km/h}$?
22.
Two persons could fit new windows in a house in $3$ days. One of the persons fell ill before the work started. How long would the job take now?
23.
A school has $8$ periods a day each of $45$ minutes duration. How long would each period be, if the school has $9$ periods a day, assuming the number of school hours to be the same?
24.
$120$ men had food provision for $200$ days. After $5$ days, $30$ men died due to an epidemic. How long will the remaining food last for the rest of the men?
25.
A garrison of $1200$ men is provisioned for $60$ days. After $15$ days, a reinforcement of $300$ men arrived. For how many days will the remaining food last?
26.
If $15$ workers can build a wall in $48$ hours, how many workers will be required to do the same work in $30$ hours?
27.
A journey by car takes $45$ minutes at $80 \text{ km/h}$. What must be the speed of the car if the journey is to be completed in $30$ minutes?
28.
$3$ pumps, working $8$ hours a day, can empty a tank in $2$ days. How many hours a day must $4$ pumps work to empty the tank in $1$ day?
29.
A girls' hostel has rations for $60$ girls for $15$ days. If $15$ girls go on a holiday, how long will the rations last for the remaining girls?
30.
It takes $8$ days for $20$ workers to harvest a field. How many workers are needed to harvest the field in $5$ days?
Section C: Time and Work
31.
$A$ can do a piece of work in $10$ days and $B$ can do it in $15$ days. In how many days will they finish it if they work together?
32.
$A$ and $B$ working together can mow a field in $5$ days. If $A$ alone can mow it in $8$ days, in how many days can $B$ alone mow it?
33.
$A$ takes $6$ hours to read a book while $B$ takes $8$ hours. If they read together, what fraction of the book will be read in $2$ hours?
34.
Tap $A$ can fill a cistern in $8$ hours and Tap $B$ can empty it in $12$ hours. If both are opened together, how long will it take to fill the cistern completely?
35.
$P$ can do a job in $24$ days. $Q$ is $20\%$ more efficient than $P$. In how many days can $Q$ alone finish the work?
36.
$A$, $B$, and $C$ can complete a piece of work in $12$, $15$, and $20$ days respectively. How long will they take to finish it if they work together?
37.
$A$ works twice as fast as $B$. If both of them can together finish a work in $12$ days, find the time $B$ alone can do it.
38.
$A$ and $B$ can do a job in $15$ days. $B$ and $C$ can do it in $20$ days. $C$ and $A$ can do it in $12$ days. In how many days can $A$, $B$, and $C$ together finish it?
39.
A cistern can be filled by tap $X$ in $4$ hours and by tap $Y$ in $6$ hours. How long will it take to fill if both taps are opened together?
40.
$45$ men can dig a canal in $16$ days. $6$ days after they started, $15$ more men joined them. How many more days will they take to complete the remaining work?
Section D: Mixed Word Problems & HOTS
41.
If $x$ and $y$ vary directly and $x=15$ when $y=20$, find $x$ when $y=32$.
42.
If $a$ and $b$ vary inversely and $a=8$ when $b=15$, find the value of $b$ when $a=12$.
43.
A train $280 \text{ m}$ long is moving at a speed of $60 \text{ km/h}$. What is the time taken by it to cross a platform $220 \text{ m}$ long?
44.
If $15$ men can reap a field in $28$ days, in how many days will $10$ men reap it?
45.
The weight of an object on the Moon is directly proportional to its weight on Earth. If an object weighing $60 \text{ kg}$ on Earth weighs $10 \text{ kg}$ on the Moon, how much will an object weighing $84 \text{ kg}$ on Earth weigh on the Moon?
46.
A wheel has $12$ spokes and the angle between a pair of consecutive spokes is $30^\circ$. If the number of spokes is increased to $15$, what will be the angle between a pair of consecutive spokes?
47.
A box of sweets is divided among $24$ children, and they get $5$ sweets each. How many would each get, if the number of the children is reduced by $4$?
48.
$5$ men or $7$ women can earn ₹$5250$ per day. How much would $7$ men and $13$ women earn per day?
49.
If $p$ varies inversely as $q$ and $p=2$ when $q=3$, find the value of $p$ when $q=0.5$.
50.
A fort had provisions for $300$ men for $90$ days. After $20$ days, $50$ men left the fort. How long will the food last at the same rate for the remaining men?
51.
A tap fills a tank in $6$ hours. After half the tank is filled, three more similar taps are opened. What is the total time taken to fill the tank completely?
52.
A variable $A$ varies directly as $B$ and inversely as $C$. When $A = 10$, $B = 5$ and $C = 2$. Find the value of $A$ when $B = 8$ and $C = 4$.
53.
The mass of a copper block varies directly with its volume. A block of volume $25 \text{ cm}^3$ has a mass of $220 \text{ g}$. What will be the volume of a block having a mass of $616 \text{ g}$?
54.
In a camp, there is a meal for $120$ men or $200$ children. If $150$ children have taken the meal, how many men will be catered to with the remaining meal?
55.
Two cogs, one with $16$ teeth and the other with $24$ teeth, are meshed together. If the smaller cog makes $45$ revolutions per minute, how many revolutions per minute does the larger cog make?
