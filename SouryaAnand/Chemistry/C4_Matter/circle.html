Section A: Basic Concepts & Pythagoras Application (1-2 Marks)
1.
How many tangents can a circle have in total? How many tangents can be drawn to a circle from a point outside it?
2.
A line intersecting a circle in two points is called a _______.
3.
A tangent $PQ$ at a point $P$ of a circle of radius $5\text{ cm}$ meets a line through the center $O$ at a point $Q$ so that $OQ = 12\text{ cm}$. Find the length of $PQ$.
4.
If tangents $PA$ and $PB$ from a point $P$ to a circle with center $O$ are inclined to each other at an angle of $80^\circ$, find $\angle POA$.
5.
The length of a tangent from a point $A$ at distance $5\text{ cm}$ from the center of the circle is $4\text{ cm}$. Find the radius of the circle.
6.
Two concentric circles are of radii $5\text{ cm}$ and $3\text{ cm}$. Find the length of the chord of the larger circle which touches the smaller circle.
7.
[Refer to Figure 1] In the given figure, if $TP$ and $TQ$ are the two tangents to a circle with center $O$ so that $\angle POQ = 110^\circ$, then find $\angle PTQ$.
8.
From a point $Q$, the length of the tangent to a circle is $24\text{ cm}$ and the distance of $Q$ from the center is $25\text{ cm}$. Find the radius of the circle.
9.
If the angle between two tangents drawn from an external point $P$ to a circle of radius $a$ and center $O$, is $60^\circ$, then find the length of $OP$.
10.
At one end $A$ of a diameter $AB$ of a circle of radius $5\text{ cm}$, tangent $XAY$ is drawn to the circle. Find the length of the chord $CD$ parallel to $XY$ and at a distance $8\text{ cm}$ from $A$.
Section B: Short Answer Applications & Angles (2-3 Marks)
11.
[Refer to Figure 2] In the given figure, $PA$ and $PB$ are tangents to the circle with center $O$ such that $\angle APB = 50^\circ$. Write the measure of $\angle OAB$.
12.
Prove that in two concentric circles, the chord of the larger circle, which touches the smaller circle, is bisected at the point of contact.
13.
Two tangents $TP$ and $TQ$ are drawn to a circle with center $O$ from an external point $T$. Prove that $\angle PTQ = 2\angle OPQ$.
14.
A quadrilateral $ABCD$ is drawn to circumscribe a circle. Prove that $AB + CD = AD + BC$.
15.
[Refer to Figure 3] In Figure 3, a circle touches all the four sides of a quadrilateral $ABCD$ whose sides are $AB = 6\text{ cm}$, $BC = 7\text{ cm}$, and $CD = 4\text{ cm}$. Find $AD$.
16.
If a hexagon $ABCDEF$ circumscribes a circle, prove that $AB + CD + EF = BC + DE + FA$.
17.
In a right triangle $ABC$ right-angled at $B$, a circle is drawn with $AB$ as diameter intersecting hypotenuse $AC$ at $P$. Prove that the tangent to the circle at $P$ bisects $BC$.
18.
Prove that the tangents drawn at the ends of a diameter of a circle are parallel.
19.
If two tangents drawn from a point $P$ to a circle with center $O$ and radius $r$ are such that angle between them is $90^\circ$, prove that $OP = \sqrt{2}r$.
20.
Two circles of radii $5\text{ cm}$ and $3\text{ cm}$ intersect at two points and the distance between their centers is $4\text{ cm}$. Find the length of the common chord.
Section C: Standard Theorems & Rigorous Proving (3 Marks)
21.
Prove that the lengths of tangents drawn from an external point to a circle are equal.
22.
Prove that the parallelogram circumscribing a circle is a rhombus.
23.
[Refer to Figure 4] A circle is touching the side $BC$ of $\triangle ABC$ at $P$ and touching $AB$ and $AC$ produced at $Q$ and $R$ respectively. Prove that $AQ = \frac{1}{2}(\text{Perimeter of } \triangle ABC)$.
24.
Prove that the angle between the two tangents drawn from an external point to a circle is supplementary to the angle subtended by the line-segment joining the points of contact at the center.
25.
$PQ$ is a chord of length $8\text{ cm}$ of a circle of radius $5\text{ cm}$. The tangents at $P$ and $Q$ intersect at a point $T$. Find the length $TP$.
26.
If an isosceles triangle $ABC$, in which $AB = AC$, is inscribed in a circle, and a tangent is drawn at $A$, prove that the tangent is parallel to $BC$.
27.
Let $s$ denote the semi-perimeter of a triangle $ABC$ in which $BC=a, CA=b, AB=c$. If a circle touches the sides $BC, CA, AB$ at $D, E, F$, respectively, prove that $BD = s - b$.
28.
Prove that the intercept of a tangent between two parallel tangents to a circle subtends a right angle at the center.
29.
Two circles touch each other externally at $C$. Prove that the common tangent at $C$ bisects the other two common tangents.
30.
Out of the two concentric circles, the radius of the outer circle is $5\text{ cm}$ and the chord $AC$ of length $8\text{ cm}$ is a tangent to the inner circle. Find the radius of the inner circle.
Section D: HOTS (Higher Order Thinking Skills) (4-5 Marks)
31.
Prove that opposite sides of a quadrilateral circumscribing a circle subtend supplementary angles at the center of the circle.
32.
[Refer to Figure 5] A triangle $ABC$ is drawn to circumscribe a circle of radius $4\text{ cm}$ such that the segments $BD$ and $DC$ into which $BC$ is divided by the point of contact $D$ are of lengths $8\text{ cm}$ and $6\text{ cm}$ respectively. Find the sides $AB$ and $AC$.
33.
In Figure 6, $XY$ and $X'Y'$ are two parallel tangents to a circle with center $O$ and another tangent $AB$ with point of contact $C$ intersecting $XY$ at $A$ and $X'Y'$ at $B$. Prove that $\angle AOB = 90^\circ$.
34.
Two tangents $PA$ and $PB$ are drawn to a circle with center $O$ from an external point $P$. Prove that $\angle APB = 2\angle OAB$.
35.
If $a, b, c$ are the sides of a right triangle where $c$ is the hypotenuse, prove that the radius $r$ of the circle which touches the sides of the triangle is given by $r = \frac{a+b-c}{2}$.
36.
In a right-angled triangle $ABC$, right-angled at $B$, a circle is inscribed touching the sides $AB, BC, CA$ at $P, Q, R$ respectively. If $AB = 10\text{ cm}$ and $BC = 24\text{ cm}$, find the radius of the incircle.
37.
Let $A$ be a point outside a circle with center $O$. A secant through $A$ cuts the circle at $B$ and $C$ ($B$ lies between $A$ and $C$). A tangent from $A$ touches the circle at $T$. Prove that $AT^2 = AB \cdot AC$. *(Note: Though Tangent-Secant theorem is often tested, it can be proved using similar triangles).*
38.
$AB$ is a diameter and $AC$ is a chord of a circle such that $\angle BAC = 30^\circ$. If the tangent at $C$ intersects $AB$ produced at $D$, prove that $BC = BD$.
39.
A circle touches the sides of a quadrilateral $ABCD$ at $P, Q, R, S$ respectively. Show that the angles subtended at the center by a pair of opposite sides are supplementary. *(Provide the detailed step-by-step proof).*
40.
Common tangents $AB$ and $CD$ to two circles intersect at $E$. Prove that $AB = CD$.
Section E: Answer Key & Hints
Ans 1: Infinite tangents. From an external point: exactly 2.
Ans 2: Secant.
Ans 3: $\sqrt{119}\text{ cm}$. Hint: $\triangle OPQ$ is right-angled at $P$. $PQ = \sqrt{12^2 - 5^2}$.
Ans 4: $50^\circ$. Hint: $\angle APB = 80^\circ$. Center lies on bisector, so $\angle APO = 40^\circ$. In $\triangle OPA$, $\angle POA = 180^\circ - 90^\circ - 40^\circ$.
Ans 5: $3\text{ cm}$. Hint: Pythagoras triplet (3, 4, 5).
Ans 6: $8\text{ cm}$. Hint: Radius to point of contact bisects chord. Half-chord = $\sqrt{5^2 - 3^2} = 4$. Total = 8.
Ans 7: $70^\circ$. Hint: $\angle PTQ + \angle POQ = 180^\circ$ (Supplementary).
Ans 8: $7\text{ cm}$. Hint: $r = \sqrt{25^2 - 24^2}$.
Ans 9: $2a$. Hint: $\angle APO = 30^\circ$. $\sin 30^\circ = \frac{OA}{OP} \implies \frac{1}{2} = \frac{a}{OP}$.
Ans 10: $8\text{ cm}$. Hint: Center $O$ is at $5\text{cm}$ from $A$. Distance of chord from $O$ is $8-5=3\text{cm}$. Half-chord = $\sqrt{5^2-3^2} = 4$. Total = 8.
Ans 11: $25^\circ$. Hint: $\triangle PAB$ is isosceles ($PA=PB$). $\angle PAB = \frac{180-50}{2} = 65^\circ$. $\angle OAB = 90^\circ - 65^\circ = 25^\circ$.
Ans 12: Hint: Draw perpendicular from center to the tangent chord. A perpendicular from center to a chord bisects it.
Ans 13: Hint: Let $\angle OPQ = x$. $\angle TPT = 90-x$. Since $TP=TQ$, $\triangle TPQ$ is isosceles. $\angle TQP = 90-x$. $\angle PTQ = 180 - (90-x + 90-x) = 2x$.
Ans 14: Hint: Use Theorem 10.2: Tangents from external points are equal. Let touching points be $P,Q,R,S$. $AP=AS, BP=BQ, CR=CQ, DR=DS$. Add them.
Ans 15: $3\text{ cm}$. Hint: $AB+CD = AD+BC \implies 6+4 = AD+7 \implies 10 = AD+7 \implies AD=3$.
Ans 16: Hint: Same logic as quadrilateral. Assign contact points $P_1, P_2 \dots P_6$. Equate tangents from vertices and add alternating sides.
Ans 17: Hint: Let tangent at $P$ intersect $BC$ at $Q$. $PQ=BQ$ (tangents from $Q$). Join $BP$. $\angle APB=90^\circ$ (angle in semicircle) $\implies \angle BPC=90^\circ$. Show $\triangle CPQ$ is isosceles to get $PQ=QC$. Thus $BQ=QC$.
Ans 18: Hint: Tangent is $\perp$ to radius. Both tangents make $90^\circ$ with the diameter. These are alternate interior angles, so lines are parallel.
Ans 19: Hint: If angle is $90^\circ$, the quadrilateral formed by tangents and radii is a square of side $r$. Diagonal $OP = \sqrt{r^2+r^2} = r\sqrt{2}$.
Ans 20: $6\text{ cm}$. Hint: The common chord is bisected at right angles by line of centers. Let distance be $x$ and $4-x$. Solve $5^2 - x^2 = 3^2 - (4-x)^2 \implies x=4$. Thus it passes through the center of smaller circle. Half-chord=3, Total=6.
Ans 21: Hint: Draw circle, external point $P$, tangents $PQ, PR$. Join $OQ, OR, OP$. Prove $\triangle OQP \cong \triangle ORP$ by RHS congruency.
Ans 22: Hint: Parallelogram: $AB=CD, AD=BC$. Circumscribing: $AB+CD=AD+BC$. So $2AB = 2AD \implies AB=AD$. All sides equal.
Ans 23: Hint: $AQ = AR$. Perimeter = $AB+BC+CA = AB+(BP+PC)+CA$. Substitute $BP=BQ, PC=CR$. Perimeter = $AB+BQ+CR+CA = AQ+AR = 2AQ$.
Ans 24: Hint: In quad $OPTQ$, $\angle OPT = \angle OQT = 90^\circ$. Sum of angles = $360^\circ$. So $\angle PTQ + \angle POQ = 180^\circ$.
Ans 25: $\frac{20}{3}\text{ cm}$. Hint: Let $TR$ intersect $PQ$ at $R$. $PR=4$. In $\triangle POR$, $OR=3$. $\triangle POR \sim \triangle PRO \implies \frac{TP}{PO} = \frac{PR}{RO} \implies \frac{TP}{5} = \frac{4}{3}$.
Ans 26: Hint: Let tangent be $XY$. $\angle XAB = \angle C$ (Alternate segment theorem, or prove via center). Since $AB=AC$, $\angle B=\angle C$. So $\angle XAB=\angle B$. Alternate interior angles equal $\implies$ parallel.
Ans 27: Hint: $s = \frac{a+b+c}{2}$. Tangents equal: $AD=AF=x, BD=BE=y, CE=CF=z$. $y+z=a, x+z=b, x+y=c$. $2(x+y+z) = 2s \implies x+y+z=s$. $BD=y=s-(x+z)=s-b$.
Ans 28: Hint: Let $AB$ be tangent intersecting parallel tangents $XY, X'Y'$. Join center to points of contact. Prove two pairs of triangles congruent. Show central angles add to $180^\circ$, so angle subtended by $AB$ is half, i.e., $90^\circ$.
Ans 29: Hint: Let common tangent at $C$ intersect direct common tangent $AB$ at $P$. Tangents from $P$ to circle 1: $PA=PC$. To circle 2: $PB=PC$. Therefore $PA=PB$, so $P$ bisects $AB$.
Ans 30: $3\text{ cm}$. Hint: $AC$ is tangent, so $\perp$ to radius. Bisects $AC \implies AD=4$. In right $\triangle ODA$, $OD = \sqrt{5^2-4^2} = 3$.
Ans 31: Hint: Join center $O$ to points of contact $P,Q,R,S$ and vertices $A,B,C,D$. This forms 8 triangles. Prove adjacent triangles congruent ($\triangle AOP \cong \triangle AOS$, etc.). Let angles be $1,2,3...8$. $\angle 1=\angle 2$, $\angle 3=\angle 4$ etc. Sum = $360^\circ \implies 2(\angle 1+\angle 4+\angle 5+\angle 8)=360^\circ \implies (\angle AOB + \angle COD) = 180^\circ$.
Ans 32: $AB = 15\text{ cm}, AC = 13\text{ cm}$. Hint: Let contact points be $D,E,F$. $AE=AF=x$. $s = 14+x$. Area via Heron's = $\sqrt{(14+x)(x)(8)(6)}$. Area via 3 triangles = $\frac{1}{2}(4)(Perimeter) = 2(28+2x) = 4(14+x)$. Equate and square: $48x(14+x) = 16(14+x)^2 \implies 3x = 14+x \implies x=7$. $AB=7+8=15, AC=7+6=13$.
Ans 33: Hint: Similar to Q28. Join $O$ to points of contact $P, Q, C$. Prove $\triangle AOP \cong \triangle AOC$ so $\angle POA = \angle AOC$. Similarly $\angle COB = \angle BOQ$. $P,O,Q$ is a diameter line. $2\angle AOC + 2\angle COB = 180^\circ \implies \angle AOB = 90^\circ$.
Ans 34: Hint: Let $\angle APB = x$. In isosceles $\triangle PAB$, $\angle PAB = 90 - x/2$. Angle between tangent and radius is $90^\circ$, so $\angle OAB = 90^\circ - (90^\circ - x/2) = x/2$. Thus $2\angle OAB = x = \angle APB$.
Ans 35: Hint: Let circle touch hypotenuse $c$ at $R$, side $a$ at $P$, side $b$ at $Q$. Quadrilateral $OPCQ$ is a square of side $r$. Tangents from vertices: $a-r$ and $b-r$. Hypotenuse $c = (a-r) + (b-r) \implies 2r = a+b-c \implies r = \frac{a+b-c}{2}$.
Ans 36: $4\text{ cm}$. Hint: Hypotenuse $AC = \sqrt{10^2+24^2} = 26$. Use formula from Q35: $r = \frac{10+24-26}{2} = \frac{8}{2} = 4$.
Ans 37: Hint: Draw $\triangle ABT$ and $\triangle ACT$. Join center to $T$ and draw perpendicular to $BC$. Prove $\triangle ABT \sim \triangle ATC$ using Alternate Segment Theorem ($\angle ATB = \angle C$) and common angle $A$. $\frac{AT}{AC} = \frac{AB}{AT} \implies AT^2 = AB \cdot AC$.
Ans 38: Hint: $\angle C$ in semicircle is $90^\circ$. $\angle B$ in $\triangle ABC$ is $60^\circ$. $\angle OBC=60^\circ$ means $\triangle OBC$ is equilateral ($\angle COB=60, \angle OCB=60$). Tangent $\angle OCD=90^\circ$. $\angle BCD = 90-60=30^\circ$. In $\triangle ABC$, exterior $\angle B=60^\circ$, so $\angle D = 60^\circ-30^\circ=30^\circ$. Since $\angle D = \angle BCD = 30^\circ$, $\triangle BCD$ is isosceles, $BC=BD$.
Ans 39: Hint: Detailed step-by-step of Q31. Required for full 5 marks in boards. State congruency criteria clearly.
Ans 40: Hint: Extend tangents to intersect at $E$. For circle 1: $EA = EC$. For circle 2: $EB = ED$. Subtract the two equations: $EA - EB = EC - ED \implies AB = CD$.
