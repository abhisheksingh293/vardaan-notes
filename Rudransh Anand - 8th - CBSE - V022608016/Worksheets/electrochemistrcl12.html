Answer Key & Solutions

Detailed Solutions for Electrochemistry Numericals
Topic A Solutions
1.
Cell representation: $\mathrm{Zn(s) | Zn^{2+}(aq) || Cu^{2+}(aq) | Cu(s)}$. Oxidation occurs at the anode (left) and reduction at the cathode (right).
2.
Lower (more negative) the standard reduction potential, higher is the reducing power. Values: $\mathrm{K} (-2.93) < \mathrm{Mg} (-2.37) < \mathrm{Cr} (-0.74) < \mathrm{Hg} (0.79) < \mathrm{Ag} (0.80)$.
Reducing power order: $\mathbf{Ag < Hg < Cr < Mg < K}$.
3.
Oxidation: $\mathrm{Cr \rightarrow Cr^{3+}}$ (Anode), Reduction: $\mathrm{Cd^{2+} \rightarrow Cd}$ (Cathode).
$\mathrm{E^\circ_{cell} = E^\circ_{cathode} - E^\circ_{anode}} = (-0.40\,\mathrm{V}) - (-0.74\,\mathrm{V}) = \mathbf{+0.34\,\mathrm{V}}$.
4.
For the reaction, $\mathrm{Ag^+}$ is reduced (Cathode) and $\mathrm{Fe^{2+}}$ is oxidized (Anode).
$\mathrm{E^\circ_{cell} = E^\circ_{Ag^+/Ag} - E^\circ_{Fe^{3+}/Fe^{2+}}} = 0.80 - 0.77 = \mathbf{0.03\,\mathrm{V}}$. Since $\mathrm{E^\circ_{cell}}$ is positive, the reaction is feasible.
5.
$\Delta_r G^\circ = -nFE^\circ_{cell}$. Here, $n=2$ (two electrons transferred).
$\Delta_r G^\circ = -2 \times 96500 \times 1.1 = -212300\,\mathrm{J\,mol^{-1}} = \mathbf{-212.3\,\mathrm{kJ\,mol^{-1}}}$.
Topic B Solutions
6.
Reaction: $\mathrm{Mg(s) + 2Ag^+(aq) \rightarrow Mg^{2+}(aq) + 2Ag(s)}$, $n=2$.
$\mathrm{E^\circ_{cell}} = 0.80 - (-2.37) = 3.17\,\mathrm{V}$.
Nernst Equation: $\mathrm{E_{cell} = E^\circ_{cell} - \frac{0.0591}{2} \log \frac{[Mg^{2+}]}{[Ag^+]^2}}$
$\mathrm{E_{cell}} = 3.17 - \frac{0.0591}{2} \log \frac{0.130}{(10^{-4})^2} = 3.17 - 0.0295 \log(1.3 \times 10^7)$
$\mathrm{E_{cell}} = 3.17 - 0.0295(7.11) = 3.17 - 0.21 = \mathbf{2.96\,\mathrm{V}}$.
7.
Reaction: $\mathrm{H^+(aq) + e^- \rightarrow \frac{1}{2}H_2(g)}$. Nernst eq: $\mathrm{E = E^\circ - \frac{0.0591}{1} \log \frac{1}{[H^+]}}$.
Given $\mathrm{pH} = 10 \implies \mathrm{[H^+] = 10^{-10}\,M}$. Since $\mathrm{E^\circ = 0\,V}$ for SHE.
$\mathrm{E = 0 - 0.0591 \log(10^{10}) = -0.0591 \times 10 = \mathbf{-0.591\,\mathrm{V}}}$.
8.
Formula: $\log K_c = \frac{n E^\circ_{cell}}{0.0591}$. Here $n=2$.
$\log K_c = \frac{2 \times 0.46}{0.0591} = \frac{0.92}{0.0591} = 15.56$.
$K_c = \text{Antilog}(15.56) = \mathbf{3.6 \times 10^{15}}$.
9.
$\Delta_r G^\circ = -nFE^\circ_{cell}$. For Daniell cell, $n=2$, $\mathrm{E^\circ = 1.1\,V}$.
$\Delta_r G^\circ = -2 \times 96500 \times 1.1 = -212300\,\mathrm{J\,mol^{-1}} = \mathbf{-212.3\,\mathrm{kJ\,mol^{-1}}}$.
10.
Concentration cell emf: $\mathrm{E_{cell} = \frac{0.0591}{n} \log \frac{C_2}{C_1}}$. Negative electrode (Anode) has $C_1 = 10^{-6}\,\mathrm{M}$. Positive electrode (Cathode) has $C_2$.
$0.118 = \frac{0.0591}{1} \log \frac{C_2}{10^{-6}} \implies 2 = \log C_2 - \log 10^{-6} \implies 2 = \log C_2 + 6$
$\log C_2 = -4 \implies C_2 = \mathbf{10^{-4}\,\mathrm{M}}$.
11.
Reaction: $\mathrm{Fe(s) + 2H^+(aq) \rightarrow Fe^{2+}(aq) + H_2(g)}$. $n=2$.
$\mathrm{E^\circ_{cell}} = 0 - (-0.44) = 0.44\,\mathrm{V}$.
$\mathrm{E_{cell} = 0.44 - \frac{0.0591}{2} \log \frac{[Fe^{2+}]}{[H^+]^2} = 0.44 - 0.0295 \log \frac{0.001}{1^2}}$
$\mathrm{E_{cell}} = 0.44 - 0.0295(-3) = 0.44 + 0.0885 = \mathbf{0.5285\,\mathrm{V}}$.
Topic C Solutions
13.
Area $A = \pi r^2 = 3.14 \times (0.5)^2 = 0.785\,\mathrm{cm^2}$. $l = 50\,\mathrm{cm}$.
1. Resistivity ($\rho$) = $\frac{R \times A}{l} = \frac{5.55 \times 10^3 \times 0.785}{50} = \mathbf{87.135\,\mathrm{\Omega\,cm}}$.
2. Conductivity ($\kappa$) = $\frac{1}{\rho} = \frac{1}{87.135} = \mathbf{0.01148\,\mathrm{S\,cm^{-1}}}$.
3. Molar Conductivity ($\Lambda_m$) = $\frac{\kappa \times 1000}{M} = \frac{0.01148 \times 1000}{0.05} = \mathbf{229.6\,\mathrm{S\,cm^2\,mol^{-1}}}$.
14.
For $0.1\,\mathrm{M\,KCl}$: $\kappa = 1.29\,\mathrm{S\,m^{-1}} = 0.0129\,\mathrm{S\,cm^{-1}}$. Cell constant $G^* = \kappa \times R = 0.0129 \times 100 = 1.29\,\mathrm{cm^{-1}}$.
For $0.02\,\mathrm{M\,KCl}$: $\kappa = \frac{G^*}{R} = \frac{1.29}{520} = \mathbf{0.00248\,\mathrm{S\,cm^{-1}}}$.
$\Lambda_m = \frac{\kappa \times 1000}{M} = \frac{0.00248 \times 1000}{0.02} = \mathbf{124\,\mathrm{S\,cm^2\,mol^{-1}}}$.
15.
By Kohlrausch's law: $\Lambda^\circ_m(\mathrm{CH_3COOH}) = \Lambda^\circ_m(\mathrm{CH_3COONa}) + \Lambda^\circ_m(\mathrm{HCl}) - \Lambda^\circ_m(\mathrm{NaCl})$
$\Lambda^\circ_m = 91 + 426 - 126 = \mathbf{391\,\mathrm{S\,cm^2\,mol^{-1}}}$.
16.
$\Lambda_m = \frac{\kappa \times 1000}{C} = \frac{4.95 \times 10^{-5} \times 1000}{0.001028} = 48.15\,\mathrm{S\,cm^2\,mol^{-1}}$.
Degree of dissociation ($\alpha$) = $\frac{\Lambda_m}{\Lambda^\circ_m} = \frac{48.15}{390.5} = 0.1233$.
Dissociation constant $K_a = \frac{C\alpha^2}{1-\alpha} \approx C\alpha^2 = 0.001028 \times (0.1233)^2 = \mathbf{1.78 \times 10^{-5}\,\mathrm{mol\,L^{-1}}}$.
18.
Formula: $\Lambda_m = \frac{\kappa \times 1000}{M} \implies \kappa = \frac{\Lambda_m \times M}{1000}$
$\kappa = \frac{138.9 \times 1.5}{1000} = \mathbf{0.20835\,\mathrm{S\,cm^{-1}}}$.
Topic D Solutions
19.
Reaction: $\mathrm{Al^{3+} + 3e^- \rightarrow Al}$. $3$ moles of electrons are needed.
Charge = $3\,\mathrm{F} = 3 \times 96500 = \mathbf{289500\,\mathrm{C}}$.
20.
In $\mathrm{MnO_4^-}$, Mn is in $+7$ state. Reaction: $\mathrm{Mn^{7+} + 5e^- \rightarrow Mn^{2+}}$.
Charge required = $\mathbf{5\,\mathrm{F}}$.
21.
$I = 5\,\mathrm{A}$, $t = 20 \times 60 = 1200\,\mathrm{s}$. Charge $Q = I \times t = 5 \times 1200 = 6000\,\mathrm{C}$.
Reaction: $\mathrm{Ni^{2+} + 2e^- \rightarrow Ni}$. $2 \times 96500\,\mathrm{C}$ deposits $58.7\,\mathrm{g}$.
Mass of Ni deposited = $\frac{58.7 \times 6000}{2 \times 96500} = \mathbf{1.825\,\mathrm{g}}$.
22.
For Ag: $\mathrm{Ag^+ + e^- \rightarrow Ag}$. Eq. wt $= 108/1 = 108$.
Mass of Ag $W = Z \times I \times t = \frac{E}{96500} \times I \times t \implies 1.45 = \frac{108}{96500} \times 1.5 \times t \implies t = \mathbf{863.7\,\mathrm{s}} \approx \mathbf{14.4\,\mathrm{min}}$.
Using Faraday's second law: $\frac{W_{Ag}}{E_{Ag}} = \frac{W_{Cu}}{E_{Cu}} = \frac{W_{Zn}}{E_{Zn}}$.
$W_{Cu} = \frac{1.45 \times 31.75}{108} = \mathbf{0.426\,\mathrm{g}}$.
$W_{Zn} = \frac{1.45 \times 32.65}{108} = \mathbf{0.438\,\mathrm{g}}$.
23.
Reaction: $\mathrm{Ca^{2+} + 2e^- \rightarrow Ca}$. $2\,\mathrm{F}$ gives $40\,\mathrm{g}$ of Ca.
To produce $20.0\,\mathrm{g}$, Faraday required = $\frac{2}{40} \times 20 = \mathbf{1\,\mathrm{F}}$.
24.
Reaction: $\mathrm{Cu^{2+} + 2e^- \rightarrow Cu}$. Eq. wt $= 63.5/2 = 31.75\,\mathrm{g}$.
$W = \frac{E \times I \times t}{96500} \implies 1.27 = \frac{31.75 \times 2 \times t}{96500} \implies t = \frac{1.27 \times 96500}{63.5} = \mathbf{1930\,\mathrm{s}} \approx \mathbf{32.16\,\mathrm{min}}$.
25.
$Q = I \times t = 0.5 \times (2 \times 3600) = 3600\,\mathrm{C}$.
Charge of one electron $e = 1.6 \times 10^{-19}\,\mathrm{C}$.
Number of electrons = $\frac{Q}{e} = \frac{3600}{1.6 \times 10^{-19}} = \mathbf{2.25 \times 10^{22}\,\text{electrons}}$.
Topic E Solutions (Theoretical Highlights)
26.
During recharging, the cell is operated like an electrolytic cell. Lead sulphate ($\mathrm{PbSO_4}$) on the anode and cathode is converted back to lead ($\mathrm{Pb}$) and lead dioxide ($\mathrm{PbO_2}$) respectively, and sulphuric acid ($\mathrm{H_2SO_4}$) is regenerated. Reaction: $\mathrm{2PbSO_4(s) + 2H_2O(l) \rightarrow Pb(s) + PbO_2(s) + 4H^+(aq) + 2SO_4^{2-}(aq)}$.
27.
Fuel cells convert chemical energy of fuel directly into electricity. Anode: $\mathrm{2H_2(g) + 4OH^-(aq) \rightarrow 4H_2O(l) + 4e^-}$. Cathode: $\mathrm{O_2(g) + 2H_2O(l) + 4e^- \rightarrow 4OH^-(aq)}$. Advantage: High efficiency (~70%) and pollution-free.
28.
A spot on the iron surface acts as an anode where oxidation occurs: $\mathrm{Fe \rightarrow Fe^{2+} + 2e^-}$. Electrons travel through the metal to another spot (cathode) where oxygen is reduced in the presence of $\mathrm{H^+}$: $\mathrm{O_2 + 4H^+ + 4e^- \rightarrow 2H_2O}$. The overall cell reaction accelerates rusting.
