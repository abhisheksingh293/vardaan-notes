Mole Concept: Conceptual Analysis
Student Name: ____________________________________ Class: Foundation / Standard Subject: Chemistry
Section A: Analytical Questions
1.
Which sample contains a greater number of molecules: 10 grams of Hydrogen gas ($\mathrm{H_2}$) or 10 grams of Oxygen gas ($\mathrm{O_2}$)? Explain your reasoning.
2.
Calculate the exact number of oxygen atoms present in $0.5$ moles of Aluminum Sulfate, $\mathrm{Al_2(SO_4)_3}$.
3.
At Standard Temperature and Pressure (STP), a certain mass of an unknown gas occupies $11.2$ Liters and weighs $14$ grams. Identify the molar mass of the gas and suggest a possible diatomic gas that matches this description.
4.
Chlorine has two naturally occurring isotopes: $\mathrm{Cl-35}$ (relative abundance $75\%$) and $\mathrm{Cl-37}$ (relative abundance $25\%$). Calculate the average atomic mass of Chlorine.
5.
An organic compound contains $40\%$ Carbon, $6.67\%$ Hydrogen, and $53.33\%$ Oxygen by mass. Determine its empirical formula.
6.
The density of liquid water is $1.0\,\mathrm{g/mL}$. How many individual water molecules are present in exactly one drop of water, assuming one drop has a volume of $0.05\,\mathrm{mL}$?
7.
Calculate the total number of electrons present in $1.6$ grams of Methane ($\mathrm{CH_4}$).
8.
If $2$ moles of Hydrogen gas ($\mathrm{H_2}$) react with $2$ moles of Oxygen gas ($\mathrm{O_2}$) to form water ($\mathrm{H_2O}$), identify the limiting reactant. Also, state how many moles of water will be formed.

Answer Key

Detailed Solutions
Solutions: Section A
1.
Ans: Molar mass of $\mathrm{H_2} = 2\,\mathrm{g/mol}$. Moles of $\mathrm{H_2} = \frac{10}{2} = 5$ moles.
Molar mass of $\mathrm{O_2} = 32\,\mathrm{g/mol}$. Moles of $\mathrm{O_2} = \frac{10}{32} = 0.3125$ moles.
Since Hydrogen gas has more moles, it contains a greater number of molecules.
2.
Ans: One molecule of $\mathrm{Al_2(SO_4)_3}$ contains $3 \times 4 = 12$ oxygen atoms. Therefore, 1 mole of the compound contains 12 moles of oxygen atoms.
$0.5$ moles of the compound will contain $0.5 \times 12 = 6$ moles of oxygen atoms.
Total oxygen atoms = $6 \times 6.022 \times 10^{23} = \mathbf{3.613 \times 10^{24} \text{ atoms}}$.
3.
Ans: At STP, $1$ mole of gas occupies $22.4\,\mathrm{L}$.
Number of moles = $\frac{11.2}{22.4} = 0.5$ moles.
Molar mass = $\frac{\text{Mass}}{\text{Moles}} = \frac{14}{0.5} = \mathbf{28\,\mathrm{g/mol}}$.
A diatomic gas with a molar mass of 28 is Nitrogen gas ($\mathrm{N_2}$).
4.
Ans: Average atomic mass = (Mass of Isotope 1 $\times$ Abundance) + (Mass of Isotope 2 $\times$ Abundance).
$= (35 \times 0.75) + (37 \times 0.25)$
$= 26.25 + 9.25 = \mathbf{35.5\,\mathrm{u}}$.
5.
Ans: Assume $100\,\mathrm{g}$ of the compound.
Moles of C = $\frac{40}{12} = 3.33$ mol.
Moles of H = $\frac{6.67}{1} = 6.67$ mol.
Moles of O = $\frac{53.33}{16} = 3.33$ mol.
Dividing by the smallest value (3.33) gives the molar ratio: C:H:O = 1:2:1.
The empirical formula is $\mathrm{CH_2O}$.
6.
Ans: Mass of one drop = Density $\times$ Volume = $1.0\,\mathrm{g/mL} \times 0.05\,\mathrm{mL} = 0.05\,\mathrm{g}$.
Molar mass of water ($\mathrm{H_2O}$) = $18\,\mathrm{g/mol}$.
Moles of water = $\frac{0.05}{18} = 0.00277$ moles.
Number of molecules = $0.00277 \times 6.022 \times 10^{23} = \mathbf{1.67 \times 10^{21} \text{ molecules}}$.
7.
Ans: Molar mass of $\mathrm{CH_4} = 12 + 4(1) = 16\,\mathrm{g/mol}$.
Moles of $\mathrm{CH_4} = \frac{1.6}{16} = 0.1$ moles.
One molecule of $\mathrm{CH_4}$ has 10 electrons (6 from Carbon, 4 from Hydrogen). Thus, 1 mole has 10 moles of electrons.
Total electrons = $0.1 \times 10 \times 6.022 \times 10^{23} = \mathbf{6.022 \times 10^{23} \text{ electrons}}$.
8.
Ans: Balanced equation: $\mathrm{2H_2 + O_2 \rightarrow 2H_2O}$.
According to the stoichiometry, $2$ moles of $\mathrm{H_2}$ require $1$ mole of $\mathrm{O_2}$. Since $2$ moles of $\mathrm{O_2}$ are available, $\mathrm{O_2}$ is in excess.
Therefore, Hydrogen ($\mathrm{H_2}$) is the limiting reactant.
Based on the limiting reactant, $2$ moles of $\mathrm{H_2}$ will produce exactly $2$ moles of water ($\mathrm{H_2O}$).
