Challenger Worksheet: Profit, Loss & Discount
Student Name: ____________________________________ Class: 8 Subject: Mathematics (ICSE)
Topic 1: Fundamentals of Profit & Loss
1.
A shopkeeper bought a chair for ₹ $850$ and sold it for ₹ $1,020$. Find his profit and profit percentage.
2.
By selling an article for ₹ $720$, a man loses $10\%$. At what price should he sell it to gain $5\%$?
3.
The cost price of $15$ articles is equal to the selling price of $12$ articles. Find the profit percentage.
4.
If the selling price of an article is $\frac{4}{3}$ of its cost price, find the profit percent.
5.
A man sells a radio for ₹ $3,450$ and makes a profit of $15\%$. Find the cost price of the radio.
6.
By selling a table for ₹ $3,220$, a shopkeeper gains $15\%$. If he had sold it for ₹ $3,000$, what would have been his profit or loss percent?
7.
An article is sold at a loss of $20\%$. Had it been sold for ₹ $150$ more, there would have been a gain of $10\%$. Find the cost price.
8.
A vendor bought lemons at $6$ for a rupee and sold them at $4$ for a rupee. Calculate his gain percent.
9.
If by selling $110$ mangoes, the cost price of $120$ mangoes is realized, find the gain percent.
10.
A property dealer sells a house for ₹ $6,30,000$ at a loss of $10\%$. What was the original cost of the house?
11.
The profit earned after selling an article for ₹ $1,516$ is the same as the loss incurred after selling the article for ₹ $1,112$. What is the cost price of the article?
12.
If the cost price is $80\%$ of the selling price, what is the profit percent?
13.
A man bought apples at $10$ for ₹ $25$ and sold them at $9$ for ₹ $25$. Find his gain or loss percent.
14.
A loss of $15\%$ gets converted into a profit of $10\%$ when the selling price is increased by ₹ $112.50$. Find the cost price.
15.
Find the selling price of an article which was bought for ₹ $2,400$ and sold at a loss of $12 \frac{1}{2}\%$.
Topic 2: Multiple Items & Overheads
16.
Rahul purchased an old scooter for ₹ $12,000$. He spent ₹ $2,500$ on its repairs and ₹ $500$ on its transport. He then sold it for ₹ $16,500$. Find his profit percent.
17.
A man buys two horses for ₹ $1,350$. He sells one so as to lose $6\%$ and the other so as to gain $7.5\%$. On the whole, he neither gains nor loses. What does each horse cost?
18.
A grocer bought $400$ eggs at ₹ $8.40$ per dozen. $40$ eggs were found broken and he sold the remaining at $90$ paise each. Find his gain or loss percent.
19.
A man sold two steel chairs for ₹ $3,960$ each. On one, he gains $10\%$ and on the other, he loses $10\%$. Find his total gain or loss percent in the whole transaction.
20.
Ravi bought $20\text{ kg}$ of rice at ₹ $45$ per kg and $30\text{ kg}$ of another variety of rice at ₹ $50$ per kg. He mixed them and sold the mixture at ₹ $52$ per kg. Find his gain percent.
21.
A dishonest dealer professes to sell his goods at cost price, but he uses a false weight of $900\text{ g}$ for a kilogram. Find his gain percent.
22.
A tradesman sold two bullocks for ₹ $8,400$ each. On one he gained $20\%$ and on the other he lost $20\%$. Find the net gain or loss amount.
23.
Raman bought $50\text{ kg}$ of apples for ₹ $3,000$. $10\%$ of the apples were spoiled. At what rate per kg should he sell the remaining apples to gain $20\%$ on the total outlay?
24.
A wholesaler buys a TV from the manufacturer for ₹ $25,000$. He spends ₹ $1,000$ on transport and sells it to a retailer at a profit of $15\%$. Find the price paid by the retailer.
25.
An article is sold at a profit of $20\%$. If both the cost price and selling price are ₹ $100$ less, the profit would be $4\%$ more. Find the cost price.
26.
A man bought two cycles for ₹ $2,500$ each. He sold one at a profit of $5\%$ and the other at a loss of $5\%$. What is his overall profit or loss percentage?
27.
A fruit seller buys oranges at $5$ for ₹ $10$ and sells them at $6$ for ₹ $15$. Find his profit percent.
28.
A dealer marks his goods $20\%$ above cost price. He then allows some discount on it and makes a profit of $8\%$. Find the rate of discount. (Hint: Link to Marked Price)
29.
Two-thirds of a consignment was sold at a profit of $5\%$ and the remainder at a loss of $2\%$. If the total profit was ₹ $400$, find the value of the consignment.
30.
By selling $33$ meters of cloth, a shopkeeper gains the selling price of $11$ meters. Find his gain percent.
Topic 3: Marked Price & Discount
31.
The marked price of a ceiling fan is ₹ $1,250$ and the shopkeeper allows a discount of $12\%$ on it. Find the selling price of the fan.
32.
A dress was sold for ₹ $1,440$ after allowing a discount of $20\%$ on its marked price. Find the marked price.
33.
Find the rate of discount when a bag marked at ₹ $450$ is sold for ₹ $360$.
34.
A shopkeeper marks his goods $30\%$ above the cost price and allows a discount of $10\%$. Find his gain percent.
35.
How much percent above the cost price should a shopkeeper mark his goods so that after allowing a discount of $25\%$ on the marked price, he gains $20\%$?
36.
The marked price of an article is ₹ $800$. A shopkeeper buys it at a discount of $20\%$ and sells it at the marked price. Find his profit percent.
37.
A tradesman allows a discount of $15\%$ on the marked price. How much above the cost price must he mark his goods to make a profit of $19\%$?
38.
A shopkeeper marks a watch at ₹ $2,800$. If he offers a discount of $15\%$ and still makes a profit of $19\%$, find the cost price of the watch.
39.
At what price should a shopkeeper mark a radio that costs him ₹ $1,200$ in order that he may offer a discount of $20\%$ and still gain $25\%$?
40.
A dealer allows his customers a discount of $15\%$ and still gains $10\%$. If the cost price of the article is ₹ $850$, find its marked price.
41.
An article is marked at ₹ $5,000$. The shopkeeper allows successive discounts of $10\%$ and $5\%$. Find the selling price.
42.
Which is a better offer for a customer: A single discount of $30\%$ or two successive discounts of $20\%$ and $10\%$? Justify your answer.
43.
After allowing a discount of $8\%$ on a toy, it is sold for ₹ $216.20$. Find the marked price of the toy.
44.
By selling an umbrella for ₹ $336$, a shopkeeper loses $4\%$. In order to gain $4\%$, what should be the selling price? (Evaluate MP if marked up first).
45.
A man bought a suitcase with a $15\%$ discount on the marked price. He sold it for ₹ $2,880$ with a $20\%$ profit on his purchase price. What was the marked price?
Topic 4: Advanced Word Problems
46.
Find the single equivalent discount for successive discounts of $10\%, 20\%,$ and $25\%$.
47.
The price of a computer is marked at ₹ $40,000$. The dealer offers successive discounts of $15\%$ and $10\%$. What is the net selling price?
48.
A company offers three types of successive discounts: (i) $25\%$ and $15\%$, (ii) $30\%$ and $10\%$, (iii) $35\%$ and $5\%$. Which offer is the best for a customer?
49.
The marked price of an article is ₹ $6,500$. After two successive discounts, it is sold for ₹ $5,265$. If the first discount is $10\%$, find the rate of the second discount.
50.
A trader marks his goods such that after allowing a discount of $12.5\%$, he makes a profit of $20\%$. Find the ratio of the cost price to the marked price.
51.
A publisher prints $2,000$ copies of a book at a cost of ₹ $70,000$. He distributes $400$ copies free as specimens. He gives a $25\%$ discount on the marked price of ₹ $75$ for the rest. Find his profit percent.
52.
A person buys an article for ₹ $p$ and sells it for ₹ $q$, gaining $r\%$. If he had sold it for ₹ $s$, he would have lost $t\%$. Express $p$ in terms of $q$ and $r$.
53.
A manufacturer sells an article to a wholesaler at a profit of $10\%$. The wholesaler sells it to a retailer at a profit of $20\%$. The retailer sells it to a customer for ₹ $56,100$ at a loss of $15\%$. Find the cost price of the manufacturer.
54.
Due to a $20\%$ fall in the rate of eggs, one can buy $2$ dozen eggs more than before by investing ₹ $160$. Find the original and the reduced rates per dozen.
55.
An article is sold at a certain price. By selling it at $\frac{2}{3}$ of that price, one loses $10\%$. Find the gain percent at the original price.
56.
A shopkeeper bought $200$ bulbs for ₹ $10$ each. $5$ bulbs were fused and had to be thrown away. The remaining were sold at ₹ $12$ each. Find the gain or loss percent.
57.
Tarun bought a TV with $20\%$ discount on the tagged price. Had he bought it with a $25\%$ discount, he would have saved ₹ $500$. At what price did he buy the TV?
58.
A man sells a plot of land for ₹ $5,40,000$, thereby losing $10\%$. At what price should he sell it in order to gain $8\%$?
59.
A watch dealer pays $10\%$ custom duty on a watch which costs ₹ $500$ abroad. He desires to make a profit of $20\%$ after giving a discount of $25\%$ to the buyer. What should be the marked price?
60.
A dishonest dealer sells his goods at $10\%$ loss on cost price but uses $20\%$ less weight. What is his actual profit or loss percentage?
