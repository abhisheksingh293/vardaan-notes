Our Constitution

1. Rules and Laws

Family is the basic unit of society. The smooth functioning of various institutions and organizations depends upon certain rules and laws recognised by a country or a community to regulate the actions of its members. We all need to follow rules and regulations to regulate our day-to-day life.

Concept Rule of Law:

It means that every citizen of the country is equal in the eyes of law. There is no discrimination on the basis of caste, religion, sex, social, or economic status. It ensures that those in power cannot act in an arbitrary or unlawful manner. Rule of law is the basic foundation of a democratic system.

2. The Constitution and Its Need

A constitution is a body of fundamental rules according to which the government of a country functions. It defines the powers and functions of various organs of the government and the rights of the citizens.

Why do we need a Constitution?

  • It defines the nature of a country's political system.
  • It lays down rules and provides safeguards against the misuse of power and abuse of authority.
  • It guarantees certain rights based on equality to establish social, political, and economic justice.
  • It ensures that minority groups are not deprived of benefits and that the majority group does not dominate the minorities.
  • It guards our national interests and does not allow dictatorial or biased decisions.

3. Vision and Values of the Indian Constitution

The Constitution of India is the result of serious deliberations of a representative body called the Constituent Assembly. The makers of the Constitution were highly concerned about feeding the starved, clothing the naked, and ending the exploitation of the deprived sections of society.

Fact The Constituent Assembly:

The Assembly included eminent personalities like Jawaharlal Nehru, Vallabhbhai Patel, Dr. Rajendra Prasad, Dr. B.R. Ambedkar, Maulana Azad, and others. It also proudly included 15 female members such as Sarojini Naidu and Rajkumari Amrit Kaur.

The Preamble

The Preamble is an introduction to the Constitution and is non-justiciable. It contains the political philosophy, aims, objectives, and basic purposes of the Constitution. The courts regard it as a key to interpret the Constitution.

Important The Soul of the Indian Constitution:

The Preamble declares India to be a SOVEREIGN SOCIALIST SECULAR DEMOCRATIC REPUBLIC. It aims to secure Justice, Liberty, Equality, and Fraternity for all its citizens. It was adopted and enacted on the twenty-sixth day of November, 1949.

4. Inspiration from Other Constitutions

The founding fathers adopted the best features from several foreign constitutions to suit Indian needs:

  • From UK (Britain): Constitutional Head of the State (like the Queen), the idea of a Preamble, the Cabinet System, Parliamentary type of Government, Bicameral Legislature, and making the Lok Sabha more powerful than the Rajya Sabha.
  • From USA: Written Constitution, the Federal System, Head of the State is called President, Provision for a Supreme Court.
  • From Canada: India is known as a "Union of States".
  • From Ireland: Concept of Directive Principles of State Policy.
  • From USSR (Russia): Fundamental Duties.

5. Basic Features of Our Constitution

A. The Lengthiest and Written Constitution

The Constitution of India is the lengthiest and most detailed constitution in the world. It is divided into 22 parts, 395 Articles, and 12 Schedules. The Constituent Assembly, presided over by Dr. Rajendra Prasad, with Dr. B.R. Ambedkar as the Chairman of the Drafting Committee, took 2 years, 11 months, and 18 days to complete its final draft.

B. Rigid and Flexible (Procedures of Amendment)

The Indian Constitution is dynamic; it is a combination of both rigid and flexible provisions. It can be amended via three methods:

  • By Simple Majority: Approved by a majority of the members present and voting in both Houses.
  • By Special Majority: Approved by absolute majority of total membership AND two-third majority of members present and voting.
  • Special Majority and Ratification: Passed by Special Majority in both Houses AND approved by the legislatures of at least half of the total states.

C. Sovereign, Socialist, Secular, and Democratic Republic

  • Sovereign: India is completely free from all external controls. No other country can dictate policies to us.
  • Socialist: India is against exploitation in all forms and strives to establish economic justice.
  • Secular: All religions enjoy equal freedom. There is no discrimination on grounds of religion.
  • Democratic: The government is elected by the people and run by their representatives.
  • Republic: The Head of the State (President) is an elected head, unlike England where the head is hereditary (King/Queen).

6. Governance and Rights

A. Parliamentary Form of Government

India adopted the British pattern of parliamentary democracy. There is a close relationship between the legislature and the executive. The President is the nominal head, but real powers are exercised by the Council of Ministers headed by the Prime Minister.

B. Federal and Unitary System

India divides powers between the Centre and the States, but features a very powerful Central (Union) Government. Therefore, India is often described as a federation with a very strong central government (unitary features).

C. Universal Adult Franchise

Every citizen of India, who is 18 years and above, is entitled to vote in the elections without any discrimination.

Fact Voting Age:

The Right to Vote was originally granted to every citizen above the age of 21 years in 1950. However, the 61st Amendment in 1988 lowered the voting age to 18 years.

D. Single Citizenship

Unlike the USA, which has double citizenship (state and national), India has Single Citizenship. Every Indian is a citizen of India, not of a specific state. All citizens can secure employment anywhere in the country.

E. Single Integrated Judicial System

India has one hierarchy of courts. The Supreme Court of India and the High Courts form a single integrated judicial structure. The judiciary is completely independent and free from the influence of the executive and legislature.
