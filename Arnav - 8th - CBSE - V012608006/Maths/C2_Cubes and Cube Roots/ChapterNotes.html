Cubes and Cube Roots

In this chapter, we will expand our understanding of exponents from square (power of 2) to exponent three. We will learn to calculate the cubes of positive and negative rational numbers, recognize perfect cubes, and extract cube roots through various methods including prime factorisation and estimation.

1. Cubes and Perfect Cubes

Concept

Cube of a Number: The cube of a number is the product obtained by multiplying the number by itself three times. It is read as the number raised to the power 3.

For a number $n$, its cube is $n^3 = n \times n \times n$.

Perfect Cube: A number $n$ is a perfect cube if there is an integer $m$ such that $n = m \times m \times m$. Example: $64 = 4 \times 4 \times 4$, so 64 is a perfect cube.

Checking for Perfect Cubes

To check if a number is a perfect cube, we express it as a product of its prime factors. If the prime factors can be grouped into triplets (groups of three identical factors) and no factor is left ungrouped, the number is a perfect cube.

Example 1: Is 216 a perfect cube?

  • Step 1: Resolve 216 into prime factors: $216 = 2 \times 2 \times 2 \times 3 \times 3 \times 3$
  • Step 2: Group them in triplets: $\underline{2 \times 2 \times 2} \times \underline{3 \times 3 \times 3} = 2^3 \times 3^3$
  • Step 3: No factor is left over. Hence, 216 is a perfect cube. $216 = (2 \times 3)^3 = 6^3$.
Important

Making a Perfect Cube: If prime factors are left ungrouped, the number is NOT a perfect cube. To make it a perfect cube:

  • Multiply by the missing factors to complete the triplet.
  • Divide by the extra left-out factors to remove them completely.

2. Properties of Cubes of Numbers

By observing the cubes of natural numbers (e.g., $2^3 = 8, 3^3 = 27, 4^3 = 64, 5^3 = 125$), we can deduce several powerful properties.

Fact File
  • Property I: The cube of an even number is always even. The cube of an odd number is always odd.
  • Property II: The cube of any multiple of 2 is always divisible by 8. (e.g., $4^3 = 64$, which is divisible by 8).
  • Property III: The cube of any multiple of 3 is always divisible by 27. (e.g., $9^3 = 729$, which is divisible by 27).
  • Property IV: The cube of a negative number is always negative. (e.g., $(-5)^3 = -5 \times -5 \times -5 = -125$).
  • Property V: The cube of a positive number is always positive.
  • Property VI: The cube of a rational number is equal to the cube of its numerator divided by the cube of its denominator: $\left(\frac{a}{b}\right)^3 = \frac{a^3}{b^3}$.

3. Cube Roots and Interesting Patterns

Concept

Cube Root: Finding the cube root is the reverse process of cubing a number. If $m$ is the cube root of $n$, we write it as $m = \sqrt[3]{n}$.

For example, since $3^3 = 27$, the cube root of 27 is 3, written as $\sqrt[3]{27} = 3$.

Fascinating Patterns Involving Cubic Numbers

  • Pattern I (Sum of Consecutive Odd Numbers): The cube of a number $n$ can be expressed as the sum of $n$ consecutive odd numbers.
    $1^3 = 1$
    $2^3 = 3 + 5$
    $3^3 = 7 + 9 + 11$
    $4^3 = 13 + 15 + 17 + 19$
  • Pattern II (Difference of Consecutive Cubes): The difference between the cubes of two consecutive numbers follows a specific formula:
    $n^3 - (n-1)^3 = 1 + n \times (n-1) \times 3$
    Example: $4^3 - 3^3 = 1 + 4 \times 3 \times 3 = 1 + 36 = 37$.
  • Pattern III (Accumulative Sums): The sequence generated by $1 + n(n+1) \times 3$ gives the sum needed to reach the next cube.
    $1^3 = 1$
    $2^3 = 1 + 7$
    $3^3 = 1 + 7 + 19$
    $4^3 = 1 + 7 + 19 + 37$

4. Finding Cube Roots

A. Cube Root by Prime Factorisation

We can find the exact cube root of a perfect cube using prime factorisation.

  1. Resolve the given perfect cube into its prime factors.
  2. Group the identical prime factors into triplets.
  3. Take one factor out of each triplet.
  4. Multiply all the factors taken from the triplets to get the cube root.

Example: Find $\sqrt[3]{2744}$

  • $2744 = \underline{2 \times 2 \times 2} \times \underline{7 \times 7 \times 7}$
  • $\sqrt[3]{2744} = 2 \times 7 = 14$
Important

Cube Root of a Negative Number: For any positive integer $x$, the cube root of $-x$ is the negative of the cube root of $x$.

Formula: $\sqrt[3]{-x} = -\sqrt[3]{x}$

Example: $\sqrt[3]{-3375} = -\sqrt[3]{3375} = -15$

B. Cube Root of Rational Numbers

If $x$ and $y$ (where $y \neq 0$) are perfect cubes, then the cube root of their fraction is the fraction of their cube roots.

Formula: $\sqrt[3]{\frac{x}{y}} = \frac{\sqrt[3]{x}}{\sqrt[3]{y}}$

Example: $\sqrt[3]{\frac{729}{1000}} = \frac{\sqrt[3]{729}}{\sqrt[3]{1000}} = \frac{9}{10}$

C. Cube Root through Estimation

If we know a number is a perfect cube, we can estimate its cube root very quickly without factorisation.

Example: Estimate the cube root of 175616

  1. Step 1: Form groups of three digits starting from the rightmost digit.
    Groups: 175 and 616
  2. Step 2: Look at the first group (616). Its unit digit is 6. Since we know $6^3 = 216$ (ends in 6), the ones digit of our required cube root must be 6.
  3. Step 3: Look at the second group (175). Find the consecutive perfect cubes it lies between. We know $5^3 = 125$ and $6^3 = 216$.
    Since $125 < 175 < 216$ (or $5^3 < 175 < 6^3$), we take the smaller number, which is 5. This is the tens digit.
  4. Result: $\sqrt[3]{175616} = 56$.
