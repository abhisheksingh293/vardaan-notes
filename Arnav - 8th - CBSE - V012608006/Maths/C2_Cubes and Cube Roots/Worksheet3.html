Section A: Advanced Theory & Logic
1.
If $n$ is a natural number ending in digit 7, prove that $n^3$ will always end in digit 3.
2.
A perfect cube can never end with 2, 3, 5, or 6 zeros. Explain the logic using prime factorization of powers of 10.
3.
Prove that the cube of any number of the form $3k+1$ is also of the form $3m+1$.
4.
If $a^3$ is even, prove that $a$ is also even. Use the concept of contrapositive logic.
5.
If $x^3$ ends in 8, find the last digit of $(x+2)^3$.
6.
Can a perfect square ever be a perfect cube? Give three examples including 1.
7.
Explain why the sum of the cubes of first $n$ natural numbers is the square of their sum.
8.
Show that if a number is multiplied by $k$, its cube is multiplied by $k^3$.
9.
If $p$ is a prime number, how many factors does $p^3$ have? List them.
10.
True or False: There exists a two-digit perfect cube whose cube root is also a perfect square. Justify.
