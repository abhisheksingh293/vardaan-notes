Rotational Symmetry

1. Quick Recall: Line Symmetry

Before diving into rotations, let's recall line symmetry. A figure has line symmetry if a line can be drawn dividing the figure into two identical halves (mirror images).

  • Regular Hexagon: Has 6 lines of symmetry.
  • Square: Has 4 lines of symmetry.
  • Circle: Has unlimited (infinite) lines of symmetry.
  • Equilateral Triangle: Has 3 lines of symmetry.
  • Scalene Triangle: Has 0 lines of symmetry.

2. Rotational Symmetry

Have you seen the hands of a clock moving? They move round and round about a fixed point, always in one direction (clockwise). Other common examples of rotating objects include the blades of a ceiling fan and the wheels of a bicycle.

Concept

What is Rotation? Rotation turns an object around a fixed point. During rotation, the size and shape of the object do not change.

  • Centre of Rotation: The fixed point about which the object rotates.
  • Angle of Rotation: The angle of turn during rotation.

Angles of Turn

  • A full turn means a rotation of $360^{\circ}$.
  • A half turn means a rotation of $180^{\circ}$.
  • A quarter turn means a rotation of $90^{\circ}$.
Fact

If an object looks exactly the same after a certain amount of rotation (less than $360^{\circ}$), we say that the object has Rotational Symmetry.

Every plane figure, if rotated through an angle of $360^{\circ}$, will coincide with itself. Thus, every figure has a rotational symmetry of order 1.

3. Order of Rotational Symmetry

In a complete turn ($360^{\circ}$), the number of times an object looks exactly the same is called the order of rotational symmetry of the object.

Example A: A Square

A square looks exactly the same 4 times during a complete $360^{\circ}$ rotation (at $90^{\circ}$, $180^{\circ}$, $270^{\circ}$, and $360^{\circ}$).

  • Centre of rotation: Intersection of its diagonals (centre of the square).
  • Angle of rotation: $90^{\circ}$
  • Order of rotation: 4

Example B: An Equilateral Triangle

An equilateral triangle looks exactly the same 3 times during a complete rotation (at $120^{\circ}$, $240^{\circ}$, and $360^{\circ}$).

  • Centre of rotation: Centroid (centre of the triangle).
  • Angle of rotation: $120^{\circ}$
  • Order of rotation: 3

Example C: A Windmill

A classic 4-blade windmill will look exactly the same after every quarter turn. Hence, it has a rotational symmetry of order 4 at an angle of $90^{\circ}$.

Practice Worksheet 1: Identify the Order
Determine the order of rotational symmetry for common shapes:
1. Regular Pentagon: Order is 5 (Angle: $\frac{360^{\circ}}{5} = 72^{\circ}$).
2. Regular Octagon: Order is 8 (Angle: $\frac{360^{\circ}}{8} = 45^{\circ}$).
3. Rectangle: Order is 2 (Looks same only at $180^{\circ}$ and $360^{\circ}$).

4. Line Symmetry AND Rotational Symmetry

When you observe shapes around us, some objects have only line symmetry, some have only rotational symmetry, and some have both.

A Circle is the most perfect symmetrical figure. It can be rotated about its centre through any angle and look the exact same. Therefore:

  • A circle has unlimited (infinite) lines of symmetry.
  • A circle has rotational symmetry around its centre for every single angle (Infinite order).
Concept

English Alphabets Analysis:

  • Only Line Symmetry: A, B, C, D, E, M, T, U, V, W, Y
  • Only Rotational Symmetry: N, S, Z (Order 2, Half-turn)
  • Both Line and Rotational Symmetry: H, I, O, X
  • No Symmetry: F, G, J, L, P, Q, R

Practice Brain Teasers & Assessment

Q1. Name one figure each having rotational symmetry of order:
(i) 4: Square (Angle: $90^{\circ}$)
(ii) 2: Rectangle or Parallelogram (Angle: $180^{\circ}$)
(iii) 3: Equilateral Triangle (Angle: $120^{\circ}$)
(iv) 6: Regular Hexagon (Angle: $60^{\circ}$)

Q2. In the word 'MATHS', which letter shows rotational symmetry of order 2?
Answer: The letters H and S. (When rotated $180^{\circ}$, they look exactly the same).

Q3. List two letters of the English alphabet which exhibit both line as well as rotational symmetry.
Answer: H, O, I, or X.

Summary: You Must Know...

  • Rotation turns an object around a fixed point.
  • The fixed point is called the centre of rotation.
  • The angle by which the object rotates about the fixed point is called the angle of rotation.
  • If an object looks exactly the same after a certain amount of rotation, it has rotational symmetry.
  • In a complete turn ($360^{\circ}$), the number of times the object looks exactly the same is called the order of rotational symmetry.
  • Order of rotational symmetry of a square is 4, and that of an equilateral triangle is 3.
