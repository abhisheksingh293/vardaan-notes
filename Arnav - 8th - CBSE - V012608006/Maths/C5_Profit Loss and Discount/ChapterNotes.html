5. Profit, Loss and Discount

In commercial mathematics, understanding how money is exchanged for goods is essential. This chapter deals with the fundamental concepts of buying and selling, calculating profits or losses, understanding how shopkeepers offer discounts to attract customers, and how Value Added Tax (VAT) is applied to purchases.

1. Recall: Profit and Loss Basics

Concept

Cost Price (C.P.): The amount paid to purchase an article or the price at which an article is made.

Selling Price (S.P.): The price at which an article is sold to the customer.

Profit: When S.P. > C.P., the difference is called profit.
$Profit = S.P. - C.P.$

Loss: When C.P. > S.P., the difference is called loss.
$Loss = C.P. - S.P.$

Profit or Loss is always calculated on the Cost Price (C.P.). Let's look at the basic formulas:

Important Formulas
  • $Profit \% = \frac{Profit}{C.P.} \times 100$
  • $Loss \% = \frac{Loss}{C.P.} \times 100$
  • $Profit = \frac{Profit \% \times C.P.}{100}$
  • $Loss = \frac{Loss \% \times C.P.}{100}$

To find S.P. when C.P. and Profit%/Loss% are given:

  • $S.P. = C.P. \times \left( \frac{100 + Profit \%}{100} \right)$
  • $S.P. = C.P. \times \left( \frac{100 - Loss \%}{100} \right)$

To find C.P. when S.P. and Profit%/Loss% are given:

  • $C.P. = \frac{S.P. \times 100}{100 + Profit \%}$
  • $C.P. = \frac{S.P. \times 100}{100 - Loss \%}$

2. Discount

When the season changes or during festivals, shopkeepers offer schemes and rebates (like 'sale upto 50%') to attract more customers and clear old stock. This rebate is called a Discount.

Fact File

Marked Price (M.P.) or List Price (L.P.): The price written or tagged on an item in a shop.

The Discount is always calculated on the Marked Price (M.P.).

Discount Formulas
  • $Selling Price (S.P.) = M.P. - Discount$
  • $Discount = M.P. - S.P.$
  • $Discount \% = \frac{Discount}{M.P.} \times 100$
  • $Discount = \frac{M.P. \times Discount \%}{100}$
  • $S.P. = M.P. \times \left[ \frac{100 - Discount \%}{100} \right]$
  • $M.P. = \frac{S.P. \times 100}{100 - Discount \%}$

3. Value Added Tax (VAT)

Concept

Value Added Tax (VAT): It is a tax on the estimated market value, added to the value of a product or material at each stage of its manufacture or distribution, which is ultimately passed on to the customer.

Whenever we purchase an item from a store, the shopkeeper adds VAT to the selling price. The customer pays the total amount.

VAT Formulas

$VAT \text{ Amount} = \frac{VAT \%}{100} \times C.P. \text{ (or Selling Price before Tax)}$

$Total Amount Paid = Price + VAT \text{ Amount}$
