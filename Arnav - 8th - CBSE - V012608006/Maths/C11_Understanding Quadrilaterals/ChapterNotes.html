Understanding Quadrilaterals

1. Introduction to Curves and Polygons

In our daily life, we come across various plane surfaces (like a notebook page or a desk top). When you join points on these surfaces without lifting your pencil and without retracing any portion, you draw a curve. Curves can be open or closed.

Concept

Polygon: A simple closed curve made up of only line segments is called a polygon.

Classification of Polygons: Polygons are classified by the number of sides (or vertices) they have:

  • 3 sides: Triangle
  • 4 sides: Quadrilateral
  • 5 sides: Pentagon
  • 6 sides: Hexagon, 7 sides: Heptagon, 8 sides: Octagon, etc.

Key Terms in a Polygon

  • Adjacent Sides: Any two sides with a common end point (vertex).
  • Adjacent Vertices: The end points of the same side of a polygon.
  • Diagonal: A line segment connecting two non-consecutive vertices of a polygon.
  • Interior vs. Exterior: A closed curve divides the plane into the interior (inside), boundary (on the curve), and exterior (outside).
Fact

Convex vs. Concave Polygons:

A polygon is convex if the line segment joining any two points inside it lies completely inside the polygon (no diagonals pass through the exterior). Polygons that are not convex are called concave.

Regular Polygons: A polygon having all sides equal (equilateral) and all angles equal (equiangular) is a regular polygon. Example: A square is a regular polygon, but a rectangle is not (sides are not all equal).

Practice Problems: Polygons

  • Q1. How many diagonals does a convex quadrilateral have? What about a convex pentagon?
  • Q2. State whether a rhombus is a regular polygon. Justify your answer.

2. Angle-Sum Properties

We know that the sum of angles of a triangle is $180^{\circ}$. By drawing diagonals from one vertex, we can divide any polygon into multiple triangles to find its total interior angle sum.

Important

Interior Angle-Sum Property:

For a Quadrilateral: It can be divided into 2 triangles. Sum $= 2 \times 180^{\circ} = 360^{\circ}$.
For a Pentagon: It can be divided into 3 triangles. Sum $= 3 \times 180^{\circ} = 540^{\circ}$.

Exterior Angle-Sum Property:

The sum of the measures of the exterior angles of any polygon (irrespective of the number of sides) is always $360^{\circ}$.

Solved Example

Find the measure of an exterior angle $x$ if the other three exterior angles of a quadrilateral are $120^{\circ}, 80^{\circ},$ and $30^{\circ}$.

Solution: We know the sum of exterior angles of any polygon is $360^{\circ}$.
$ 120^{\circ} + x + 80^{\circ} + 30^{\circ} = 360^{\circ} $
$ 230^{\circ} + x = 360^{\circ} $
$ x = 360^{\circ} - 230^{\circ} = 130^{\circ} $

^{\circ} - 230^{\circ} = 130^{\circ} $$

Practice Problems: Angle Sum

  • Q1. The four angles of a quadrilateral are in the ratio $3 : 5 : 7 : 9$. Find the measure of each angle.
  • Q2. Find the measure of each interior angle of a regular hexagon.

3. Types of Quadrilaterals: Trapezium and Parallelogram

Concept

Trapezium: A quadrilateral in which exactly one pair of opposite sides is parallel.

Parallelogram: A quadrilateral in which both pairs of opposite sides are parallel.

Note: Every parallelogram is a trapezium, but a trapezium is not necessarily a parallelogram.

Important

Properties of a Parallelogram:

  1. Opposite angles are equal (e.g., $\angle A = \angle C$ and $\angle B = \angle D$).
  2. Opposite sides are equal in length ($\overline{AB} = \overline{CD}$ and $\overline{BC} = \overline{AD}$).
  3. Diagonals bisect each other (they cut each other into two equal halves).
  4. Adjacent angles are supplementary (sum to $180^{\circ}$).

Practice Problems: Parallelograms

  • Q1. Two adjacent angles of a parallelogram are in the ratio $1 : 5$. Find all the angles of the parallelogram.
  • Q2. The perimeter of a parallelogram is $110\text{ cm}$. If two adjacent sides are in the ratio $3 : 8$, find the lengths of the sides.

4. Special Parallelograms: Rhombus, Rectangle, and Square

When a parallelogram satisfies additional specific conditions, it gets a special name.

Fact

Rhombus: A parallelogram having a pair of adjacent sides equal. As a result, all sides of a rhombus are equal.

  • It has all the properties of a parallelogram.
  • Special Property: The diagonals of a rhombus bisect each other at right angles ($90^{\circ}$).
Concept

Rectangle: A parallelogram in which one angle is a right angle ($90^{\circ}$). Consequently, each angle of a rectangle is $90^{\circ}$.

  • It has all the properties of a parallelogram.
  • Special Property: The diagonals of a rectangle are equal in length.
Important

Square: A square is a parallelogram in which one pair of adjacent sides is equal AND one angle is a right angle. It is both a rhombus and a rectangle!

Properties of a Square:

  • All sides are equal.
  • Each angle is $90^{\circ}$.
  • Diagonals are equal in length.
  • Diagonals bisect each other at right angles ($90^{\circ}$).

Practice Problems: Special Parallelograms

  • Q1. The diagonals of a rhombus are in the ratio $3 : 4$. If its perimeter is $40\text{ cm}$, find the lengths of the sides and the diagonals of the rhombus.
  • Q2. A pair of adjacent sides of a rectangle are in the ratio $3 : 4$. If its diagonal is $20\text{ cm}$, find the lengths of the sides and the perimeter of the rectangle.

5. Lines of Symmetry (Appendix)

A line of symmetry divides a figure into two congruent halves that exactly overlap when folded.

  • Rectangle: 2 lines of symmetry (lines joining the mid-points of opposite sides).
  • Rhombus: 2 lines of symmetry (the two diagonals).
  • Square: 4 lines of symmetry (the two diagonals AND the lines joining mid-points of opposite sides).
  • Parallelogram (general): 0 lines of symmetry.
  • Kite: 1 line of symmetry (one diagonal).

6. Chapter Summary (You Must Know)

  • A simple closed curve made of line segments is a polygon.
  • A polygon with all sides and angles equal is a regular polygon.
  • The sum of the interior angles of a quadrilateral is $360^{\circ}$.
  • The sum of the exterior angles of ANY polygon is $360^{\circ}$.
  • A trapezium has one pair of parallel sides; a parallelogram has two.
  • In a parallelogram: opposite sides are equal, opposite angles are equal, and diagonals bisect each other.
  • A rhombus has equal sides and diagonals that bisect at right angles.
  • A rectangle has equal diagonals and $90^{\circ}$ angles.
  • A square combines all properties of rectangles and rhombuses.
