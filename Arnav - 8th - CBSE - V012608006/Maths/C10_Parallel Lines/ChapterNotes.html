Parallel Lines

1. Introduction to Parallel Lines

In our daily lives, we come across several examples of lines that never meet. Whether it is the railway tracks, the tracks made by the wheels of a car on a wet muddy road, or the opposite edges of your math book, they all share a common property.

Concept

Parallel Lines: If two lines in a plane do not intersect each other even when extended infinitely in both directions, they are called parallel lines. We denote parallel lines $l$ and $m$ as $l \parallel m$.

Transversal: A line that intersects two or more lines at distinct points is called a transversal.

Angles Formed by a Transversal

When a transversal intersects two lines, 8 angles are formed. These angles are categorized into distinct pairs:

  • Corresponding Angles: Angles in the same relative position at each intersection where the straight line crosses the others.
  • Alternate Interior Angles: A pair of angles on opposite sides of the transversal and between the two lines.
  • Alternate Exterior Angles: A pair of angles on opposite sides of the transversal and outside the two lines.
  • Interior Angles on the Same Side (Co-interior): A pair of angles on the same side of the transversal and between the two lines.
Fact

Properties of Parallel Lines:

If two parallel lines are cut by a transversal, then:

  • The pairs of corresponding angles are equal.
  • The pairs of alternate (interior as well as exterior) angles are equal.
  • The interior angles on the same side of the transversal are supplementary (their sum is $180^\circ$).

Practice Problems: Angles

  • Q1. Let $l \parallel m$. If one angle formed by the transversal is $55^\circ$, find the corresponding angle, the alternate interior angle, and the co-interior angle to it.
  • Q2. Two parallel lines are intersected by a transversal. If a pair of interior angles on the same side are $2x$ and $3x - 20^\circ$, find the value of $x$.

2. Conditions for Parallelism (Converse)

Is the reverse of the above properties true? Yes! If you are given two lines and a transversal, you can prove the lines are parallel by checking their angles.

Important

Converse Properties:

If a transversal cuts a pair of lines in such a way that ANY ONE of the following is true, then the lines are PARALLEL:

  1. Any pair of corresponding angles are equal.
  2. Any pair of alternate interior (or exterior) angles are equal.
  3. Any pair of interior angles on the same side of the transversal are supplementary (sum to $180^\circ$).

Solved Example

Example: In a quadrilateral $ABCD$, $\angle A = 90^\circ$ and $\angle B = 90^\circ$. Is $\overline{AD} \parallel \overline{BC}$?

Solution:
Line segment $\overline{AB}$ acts as a transversal intersecting $\overline{AD}$ and $\overline{BC}$.
We are given $\angle A = 90^\circ$ and $\angle B = 90^\circ$.
The sum of interior angles on the same side of the transversal is: $$ \angle A + \angle B = 90^\circ + 90^\circ = 180^\circ $$
Since the sum is $180^\circ$ (they are supplementary), according to the converse property, $\overline{AD} \parallel \overline{BC}$.

3. Distance Between Parallel Lines

If you measure the breadth of your study table at different points, you will find it is exactly the same everywhere. This illustrates a fundamental property of parallel lines.

Concept

Distance Property: The perpendicular distance between two parallel lines does not change; it is the same everywhere.

Lines Parallel/Perpendicular to a Given Line

  • Parallel to the Same Line: Lines parallel to the same given line are parallel to each other. If $m \parallel l$ and $n \parallel l$, then $m \parallel n$.
  • Perpendicular to the Same Line: Two lines in the same plane perpendicular to a given line are parallel to each other. (Because they form corresponding angles of $90^\circ$ which are equal).

Practice Problems: Distance & Multi-lines

  • Q1. Lines $l, m, n$ are parallel to each other. A transversal $p$ intersects them. If the angle corresponding to line $l$ is $110^\circ$, what is the corresponding angle on line $n$?
  • Q2. If $p \perp l$ and $q \perp l$, prove that $p \parallel q$.

4. Division of a Line Segment into Equal Parts

Using the properties of parallel lines, we can divide a line segment into $n$ equal parts or in a specific internal ratio without just measuring and guessing.

Fact

Construction Concept:

If $\overline{AB}$ is any line segment and '$n$' is a positive integer, $\overline{AB}$ can be divided into $n$ equal parts by drawing parallel lines. This relies on the property that equal intercepts on parallel lines create equal intercepts on any transversal cutting them.

Dividing a Line Segment internally in a ratio (e.g., $4:3$)

Steps of Construction:

  1. Draw a line segment $\overline{AB} = 6 \text{ cm}$.
  2. Draw rays $\overrightarrow{AQ}$ and $\overrightarrow{BP}$ parallel to each other on opposite sides of $\overline{AB}$.
  3. Add the ratio numbers: $4 + 3 = 7$. Mark 7 points on $\overrightarrow{AQ}$ ($A_1$ to $A_7$) and 7 points on $\overrightarrow{BP}$ ($B_1$ to $B_7$) at equal distances.
  4. Join the final points $A_7$ to $B$ and $B_7$ to $A$.
  5. To divide in the ratio $4:3$, join the point $A_4$ to $B_3$. The intersection of this line with $\overline{AB}$ gives a point $C$.
  6. Point $C$ divides $\overline{AB}$ internally in the ratio $4:3$.

Practice Problems: Constructions

  • Q1. Draw a line segment $\overline{AB} = 7.6 \text{ cm}$. Divide it into 4 equal parts. What is the length of each part?
  • Q2. Draw a line segment of length $5.5 \text{ cm}$. Divide it internally in the ratio $2:3$.
  • Q3. Draw a line segment $\overline{AB} = 7 \text{ cm}$ and find a point $P$ on it such that $\overline{AP} : \overline{PB} = 2:3$.

5. Chapter Summary (You Must Know)

  • Lines parallel to the same line are parallel to each other.
  • Lines perpendicular to the same line are parallel to each other.
  • If two parallel lines are cut by a transversal, corresponding angles are equal, alternate angles are equal, and co-interior angles are supplementary.
  • Division of a line segment into '$n$' equal parts can be reliably done by constructing parallel lines.
  • A line segment can be divided internally in a given ratio by constructing parallel lines and using the intercept theorem logic.
