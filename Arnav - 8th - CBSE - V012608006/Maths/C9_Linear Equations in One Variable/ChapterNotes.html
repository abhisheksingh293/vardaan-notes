Linear Equations in One Variable

1. Introduction to Linear Equations

Before diving into complex problem-solving, let's brush up on the fundamentals of equations and linear equations.

Concept

Equation: An equation is a mathematical statement of equality between two algebraic expressions involving an unknown quantity called a variable.

Linear Equation in One Variable: A linear equation in one variable is an equation in which the highest power (degree) of the variable is exactly one.

Fact

The standard form of a linear equation in one variable is given as:

$$ px + q = 0 $$

where $p$ and $q$ are rational numbers, and $p \neq 0$. The solution (or root) of this equation is $$ x = -\frac{q}{p} $$

Rules for Solving Linear Equations

Remember that an equation behaves like a weighing scale. It remains balanced (does not change) when you apply the following operations to both sides:

  • Add the same number to both sides of the equation.
  • Subtract the same number from both sides of the equation.
  • Multiply or divide both sides of the equation by the same non-zero number.
  • Transpose: You can shift any term from one side of the equality sign to the other, provided you change its sign ($+$ becomes $-$, and vice versa).

2. Equations Reducible to Linear Form

Often, equations are not given in a simple linear form. In this chapter, we specifically focus on equations of the type:

$$ \frac{ax + b}{cx + d} = k $$

Where $a$, $b$, $c$, $d$, and $k$ are given numbers, $x$ is the variable, and the denominator $cx + d \neq 0$. Such equations can be converted into standard linear equations using the Cross Multiplication Method.

Important

The Cross Multiplication Method

If you have an equation of the form $$ \frac{ax+b}{cx+d} = \frac{m}{n} $$ you can eliminate the fractions by cross-multiplying:

  1. Multiply the numerator of the L.H.S. by the denominator of the R.H.S.: $n(ax+b)$
  2. Multiply the numerator of the R.H.S. by the denominator of the L.H.S.: $m(cx+d)$
  3. Equate both expressions: $$ n(ax+b) = m(cx+d) $$

This transforms the problem into a simple linear equation!

Solved Example

Solve the equation: $$ \frac{2x - 1}{3x + 5} = 5 $$

Solution:
1. Treat $5$ as $\frac{5}{1}$. By applying cross multiplication, we get:
$$ 1(2x - 1) = 5(3x + 5) $$
2. Expand the brackets:
$$ 2x - 1 = 15x + 25 $$
3. Transpose the variable terms to one side and constants to the other:
$$ 2x - 15x = 25 + 1 $$
$$ -13x = 26 $$
4. Solve for $x$:
$$ x = \frac{26}{-13} = -2 $$

Practice Problems: Solving Equations

  • Q1. Solve: $$ \frac{4z - 3}{2z + 1} = \frac{5}{7} $$
  • Q2. Solve: $$ \frac{3x}{5x - 5} = -1 $$
  • Q3. Find the positive value of $x$ for which: $$ \frac{x^2 - 9}{5 + x^2} = \frac{5}{9} $$ (Hint: Substitute $x^2 = y$ temporarily to solve)

3. Applications of Linear Equations (Word Problems)

Mathematical modeling is the process of translating real-world problems into mathematical equations. Linear equations are exceptionally useful for solving everyday problems related to numbers, ages, speed-distance-time, and geometry.

Concept

Steps to Solve Word Problems:

  • Step I: Read the problem carefully. Identify the knowns and the unknown quantity required.
  • Step II: Denote the unknown quantity by a variable like $x$, $y$, or $z$.
  • Step III: Translate the English statements step-by-step into mathematical expressions.
  • Step IV: Use the conditions given in the problem to formulate an equation.
  • Step V: Solve the equation.
  • Step VI (Crucial): Verify your answer by plugging it back into the original word problem's conditions.

Key Application Types

A. Age Problems

If the ratio of present ages of A and B is $m:n$, assume their ages as $mx$ and $nx$. For future ages, add the years. For past ages, subtract the years.

B. Two-Digit Number Problems

This is a highly tested concept. Any two-digit number consists of a "tens digit" and a "ones digit".

If the ones digit is $x$ and the tens digit is $y$, then:
Original Number = $$ 10 \times (\text{tens digit}) + (\text{ones digit}) = 10y + x $$
Reversed Number (interchanging digits) = $$ 10x + y $$

C. Consecutive Multiples

If you need to find consecutive multiples of a number (say, 8), let the first multiple be $8x$. The next consecutive multiples will be $8(x+1)$ and $8(x+2)$.

Alternative approach: Let the multiples be $y$, $y+8$, and $y+16$.

D. Speed, Distance, and Time (Boats and Streams)

When an object (like a steamer or boat) travels in water, the speed of the water current affects its total speed.

  • Downstream (Along the current): Speed = (Speed of Boat in still water) $+$ (Speed of Stream)
  • Upstream (Against the current): Speed = (Speed of Boat in still water) $-$ (Speed of Stream)
  • Remember the formula: $$ \text{Distance} = \text{Speed} \times \text{Time} $$
Fact

Example (Boats): A steamer goes downstream from one port to another in 6 hours. It covers the same distance upstream in 7 hours. If the speed of the stream is $2 \text{ km/hr}$, find the speed of the steamer in still water.

Solution: Let speed of steamer be $x \text{ km/hr}$.
Downstream Speed = $x + 2$. Distance covered = $6(x + 2)$
Upstream Speed = $x - 2$. Distance covered = $7(x - 2)$
Since the distance is identical:
$$ 6(x + 2) = 7(x - 2) $$
$$ 6x + 12 = 7x - 14 $$
$$ -x = -26 \implies x = 26 \text{ km/hr} $$

Practice Problems: Word Applications

  • Q1 (Ages): Two years ago, a father was three times as old as his son and two years hence, twice his age will be equal to five times that of his son. Find their present ages.
  • Q2 (Digits): The sum of the digits of a 2-digit number is 8. The number obtained by interchanging the digits exceeds the given number by 18. Find the given numbers.
  • Q3 (Fractions): The denominator of a rational number is greater than its numerator by 7. If 3 is subtracted from the numerator and 2 is added to its denominator, the new number becomes $\frac{1}{5}$. Find the rational number.
  • Q4 (Trains): The distance between 2 towns is 300 km. Two cars start simultaneously from these towns and move towards each other. The speed of one car is more than the other by $7 \text{ km/hr}$. If the distance between the cars after 2 hours is 34 km, find the speed of the cars.
  • Q5 (Mensuration): The length of a rectangle is greater than the breadth by 18 cm. If both length and breadth are increased by 6 cm, then the area increases by $168 \text{ cm}^2$. Find the length and breadth of the rectangle.

4. Chapter Summary (You Must Know)

  • An equation is a statement of equality of two algebraic expressions involving an unknown quantity called a variable.
  • An equation in which the highest power of the variable is one is called a linear equation.
  • If an equation is in the format $\frac{ax+b}{cx+d} = k$, using the cross multiplication method yields $1 \times (ax+b) = k \times (cx+d)$, which converts it into a solvable linear form.
  • When forming word problem equations, read the text carefully to correctly identify the mathematical relationships (ratios, differences, sums) between the unknown variable and the given data.
