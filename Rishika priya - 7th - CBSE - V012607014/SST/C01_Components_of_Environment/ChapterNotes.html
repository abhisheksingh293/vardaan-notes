Components of Environment

1. Understanding the Environment

Environment in its true sense can be defined as all that surrounds us. It is our basic life support system as it provides the air we breathe, the water we drink, the food we eat, and the land where we live.

The environment has two major types of elements:

  • Natural Environment: This includes the Physical environment (non-living things like land, water, and air) and the Biological environment (all living things like plants and animals). Human beings are the most important component of the biological environment.
  • Man-made Environment: A large variety of man-made things necessary for our living.
Concept

Interdependence of Environments

The two components of the natural environment (physical and biological) are largely interdependent. Any major change in the physical environment brings a change in the accompanying biological environment.

Changes can be gradual (like the development of soil over years) or sudden (like the occurrence of an earthquake or volcano).

2. Four Spheres of Environment

Our natural environment on Mother Earth can be broadly divided into four unique spheres that support life:

A. Lithosphere

The literal meaning of 'lithosphere' is the 'sphere of rocks'. It is the earth's solid outer layer, providing us with land and mineral wealth. It is about 100 km in thickness.

  • Sial: The uppermost part of the lithosphere composed of rocks rich in silica and aluminium.
  • Sima: The layer below the sial, rich in silica and magnesium, generally found below ocean basins.

B. Atmosphere

The literal meaning of 'atmosphere' is the 'sphere of air'. It is a gaseous envelope extending about 1,600 km above the earth's surface.

It is the most dynamic sphere where sudden changes affect atmospheric conditions. Of its total mass, 99% exists within 32 km of height from the earth's surface.

C. Hydrosphere

The 'sphere of water' includes oceans, rivers, lakes, and seas. Earth is often called the Water Planet or Blue Planet due to the abundance of water.

Fact

Water Distribution

  • Water covers 71% of the earth's surface.
  • Distribution is not uniform: 43% of water lies in the Northern Hemisphere and 57% in the Southern Hemisphere.
  • Oceans hold 96.5% of all water on Earth.

D. Biosphere

The word 'biosphere' means the 'sphere of life'. It is the narrow zone of contact between all the other three spheres where all kinds of living organisms (plants, animals, and microbes) exist.

3. Biodiversity and Wildlife Conservation

The earth is full of diversities in land, soil, plants, and animals. The process of diversification over long periods has resulted in the emergence of new species, creating biodiversity.

Important

Wildlife Protection Act, 1972

The Government of India passed this Act to protect and conserve biodiversity. Under this law, poaching and hunting are prohibited and are punishable crimes.

Currently, there are 102 National Parks and 515 Wildlife Sanctuaries in India to protect critically endangered species like the Forest Owlet, Ganges Shark, Tiger Toad, Flying Frog, and Leatherback Turtle.

4. Important Keywords to Remember

  • Biodiversity: A variety of life on earth comprising plants, animals, and micro-organisms.
  • Microbes: Very small organisms.
  • National Park: A well-demarcated reserved area for the protection of wildlife. It does not allow human activity.
  • Ocean: Vast expanse of saline water on the earth's surface.
  • Wildlife Sanctuary: A geographical area meant for the conservation of biodiversity. Limited human activities are permitted here.
