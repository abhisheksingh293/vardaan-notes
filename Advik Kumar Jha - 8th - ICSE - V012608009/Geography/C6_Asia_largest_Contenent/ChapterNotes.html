ASIA: THE LARGEST CONTINENT

1. Introduction to Asia

Asia is the largest continent in the world, covering approximately one-third of the total land area of the Earth. It holds the distinction of being the cradle of human civilisation and is known for its great river valley civilisations, immense natural diversity, and vast treasure of resources.

It has the highest mountains and plateaus, the largest stretches of lowlands, the coldest and hottest places, and the most thickly populated regions of the world.

Concept

Location & Boundaries

  • Extent: Spans from roughly 10°S to 80°N latitudes and from 24°E to 169°W longitudes. Due to its vast longitudinal extent, it has the greatest number of time zones.
  • Oceans: Surrounded by the Indian Ocean (South), Pacific Ocean (East), and Arctic Ocean (North).
  • Land Boundaries: Separated from Europe by the Ural Mountains, the Ural River, and the Caspian Sea. The combined landmass is called Eurasia. It is separated from Africa by the narrow Isthmus of Suez and the Red Sea.

2. Political Divisions

Asia comprises diverse countries, grouped politically into various sub-regions:

  • East Asian Countries (The Far East): Japan, North Korea, South Korea, China, Taiwan, and Mongolia.
  • South-east Asian Countries: Myanmar, Malaysia, Thailand, Singapore, Cambodia, Vietnam, Laos, Indonesia, East Timor, Philippines, and Brunei.
  • South Asian Countries: India, Pakistan, Afghanistan, Bangladesh, Sri Lanka, Nepal, Bhutan, and Maldives.
  • Southwest Asian Countries (Middle East): Saudi Arabia, Iran, Turkey, Oman, Bahrain, UAE, Kuwait, Iraq, Syria, Israel, Lebanon, Qatar, Yemen, Jordan.
  • Central Asia: Formed after the collapse of the USSR in 1991, including Kazakhstan, Turkmenistan, Uzbekistan, Kyrgyzstan, and Tajikistan.
Fact

Asia Fact File

  • Highest Mountain: Mt. Everest (The Himalayas, 8,848 m)
  • Longest River: River Yangtze (5,464 km)
  • Largest Country by Area: Russia
  • Smallest Country by Area: Maldives

3. Physiography (Major Physical Divisions)

The continent of Asia can be broadly divided into distinct physical divisions:

A. Central Highlands

An extensive area covered by mountains and plateaus. The Pamir Knot (Roof of the World) sits to the north of India, from which major mountain ranges radiate:

  • South-east: Great Himalayas and Karakoram range.
  • West: Hindukush range.
  • East: Tien Shan, Altai, and Yablonovy ranges.

These mountains enclose intermontane plateaus like the Plateau of Tibet, the Plateau of Iran, and the Anatolian Plateau (Turkey). Mid-latitude deserts like the Gobi Desert and Taklamakan Desert are also located here.

B. Northern Lowlands

Lying north of the Central Mountain Belt, it primarily consists of:

  • Great Siberian Plain: Drained by the Ob, Yenisey, and Lena rivers flowing into the Arctic Ocean.
  • Central Asia Lowlands: An area of inland drainage. The Amu Darya and Syr Darya rivers empty into the Aral Sea here.

C. Plateaus & River Basins

  • Old Plateaus: Made up of old hard rocks, including the Arabian Plateau, the Deccan Plateau (India), and the Yunnan-Guizhou Plateau (China).
  • River Basins: Highly fertile areas such as the Mesopotamian lowland (drained by Euphrates and Tigris), the Indo-Gangetic Plain (Ganga, Brahmaputra, Indus), and Chinese plains (Hwang Ho, Yangtze).

4. Climate of Asia

Asia experiences extreme climatic contrasts, from the hottest places like Jacobabad to the coldest like Verkhoyansk in Siberia. The climate is affected by its latitudinal extent, relief features (like the Himalayas blocking cold Arctic winds), distance from the sea, ocean currents, and wind systems (like monsoons and jet streams).

Important

Major Climatic Types

  • Tundra Climate: Northern Siberia. Long, extremely cold winters; very short, cool summers.
  • Cool Temperate Continental (Taiga): Central Siberia. Bitterly cold winters, short summers. Convectional rain in summer.
  • Steppes Type: Around the Caspian Sea. Hot summers, cold winters, low rainfall.
  • Mediterranean Type: West Asia (Turkey, Syria). Hot, dry summers; mild, wet winters.
  • Tropical Hot Desert: Arabian Peninsula, Thar Desert. Extremely hot summers, very cold/dry winters, scant rainfall (< 25 cm).
  • Tropical Monsoon: India, Southeast Asia. Distinct seasons with heavy summer rainfall brought by monsoon winds.
  • Equatorial Climate: Indonesia, Malaysia. Hot and wet year-round, no dry season.

5. Natural Vegetation and Wildlife

The vast climatic diversity leads to varied vegetation and wildlife across the continent:

  • Tundra Vegetation: Found in the Arctic coast. Features lichens and short bushes that appear during the short summer melt. Wildlife: Reindeer, polar bear, Arctic fox.
  • Taiga (Coniferous Forest): Found between 50°N and the Arctic Circle. Tall evergreen trees with needle-shaped leaves like fir, spruce, and pine (valuable softwood).
  • Tropical Deciduous Forest: India and Southeast Asia. Trees shed their leaves during the long dry season. Key trees include teak, sal, and sandalwood. Wildlife: Elephants are commonly found here.
  • Tropical Rainforest (Equatorial): Malaysia and Indonesia. Dense, evergreen forests with a thick canopy. Hardwood trees like rosewood, ebony, and mahogany. Wildlife: Monkeys, birds, butterflies, and diverse reptiles.
  • Thorny Desert Vegetation: Southwest and Central Asia (Arabian, Thar, Gobi deserts). Features thorny scrubs, bushes, clumped grasses, and date palms. Wildlife: Camel, gazelle, and cheetah.
