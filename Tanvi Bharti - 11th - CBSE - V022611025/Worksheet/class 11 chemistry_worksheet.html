Some Basic Concepts of Chemistry
Student Name: ____________________________________ Class: 11 Subject: Chemistry
Section A: Gay Lussac's Law & Avogadro's Law
1.
50 mL of hydrogen reacts with 50 mL of chlorine to form hydrogen chloride gas. Calculate the volume of hydrogen chloride gas produced at the same temperature and pressure.
2.
100 mL of ethene ($\mathrm{C_2H_4}$) is burnt completely in oxygen. Calculate the volume of oxygen required and the volume of carbon dioxide formed under identical conditions.
3.
If 20 mL of carbon monoxide is mixed with 20 mL of oxygen and the mixture is ignited, what will be the volume of the resulting gases at the same temperature and pressure?
4.
A vessel contains 2.5 liters of nitrogen gas at STP. Calculate the number of moles and the number of molecules present in the vessel.
5.
Calculate the volume occupied by 0.5 moles of ammonia gas ($\mathrm{NH_3}$) at Standard Temperature and Pressure (STP).
6.
Find the mass of 11.2 liters of $\mathrm{CO_2}$ gas at STP.
Section B: The Mole Concept
1.
Calculate the number of moles in 90 g of water ($\mathrm{H_2O}$).
2.
Calculate the mass of 2.5 moles of sulfuric acid ($\mathrm{H_2SO_4}$).
3.
How many molecules are present in 8.0 g of oxygen gas ($\mathrm{O_2}$)?
4.
Calculate the number of atoms present in 0.1 moles of carbon ($\mathrm{C}$).
5.
What is the mass of exactly 1 molecule of carbon dioxide ($\mathrm{CO_2}$) in grams?
6.
Calculate the total number of electrons present in 1.6 g of methane ($\mathrm{CH_4}$).
7.
Find the number of moles of calcium carbonate ($\mathrm{CaCO_3}$) present in 50 g of the substance.
8.
Determine the volume occupied by 14 g of nitrogen gas ($\mathrm{N_2}$) at STP.
9.
How many atoms of hydrogen are there in 34 g of ammonia ($\mathrm{NH_3}$)?
Section C: Percentage Composition
1.
Calculate the mass percentage of carbon and hydrogen in methane ($\mathrm{CH_4}$).
2.
Determine the mass percentage of water of crystallization in blue vitriol ($\mathrm{CuSO_4 \cdot 5H_2O}$). (Atomic masses: $\mathrm{Cu}=63.5$, $\mathrm{S}=32$, $\mathrm{O}=16$, $\mathrm{H}=1$)
3.
Calculate the percentage of nitrogen in urea ($\mathrm{NH_2CONH_2}$).
4.
What is the percentage composition of oxygen in potassium chlorate ($\mathrm{KClO_3}$)? (Atomic masses: $\mathrm{K}=39$, $\mathrm{Cl}=35.5$, $\mathrm{O}=16$)
Section D: Empirical & Molecular Formula
1.
An organic compound contains 80% carbon and 20% hydrogen. Determine its empirical formula.
2.
A compound contains 40.0% carbon, 6.67% hydrogen, and 53.33% oxygen by mass. Find its empirical formula.
3.
The empirical formula of a compound is $\mathrm{CH_2O}$. If its molar mass is $180\mathrm{~g/mol}$, determine its molecular formula.
4.
An oxide of iron contains 69.9% iron and 30.1% oxygen by mass. What is the empirical formula of the oxide? (Atomic masses: $\mathrm{Fe}=55.85$, $\mathrm{O}=16$)
Section E: Stoichiometry (Including Limiting Reagent)
1.
Calculate the mass of water produced by the complete combustion of 16 g of methane.
2.
If 12 g of carbon is burnt in the presence of 32 g of oxygen, calculate the mass of carbon dioxide formed.
3.
3.0 g of $\mathrm{H_2}$ reacts with 29.0 g of $\mathrm{O_2}$ to yield water. Identify the limiting reagent and calculate the maximum mass of water that can be formed.
4.
Calculate the volume of $\mathrm{CO_2}$ gas evolved at STP by heating 20 g of pure limestone ($\mathrm{CaCO_3}$).
5.
How many grams of $\mathrm{HCl}$ are required to react completely with 8.7 g of $\mathrm{MnO_2}$? (Reaction: $\mathrm{MnO_2} + 4\mathrm{HCl} \rightarrow \mathrm{MnCl_2} + 2\mathrm{H_2O} + \mathrm{Cl_2}$) ($\mathrm{Mn}=55$)
Section F: Principle of Atom Conservation (POAC)
1.
27.6 g of silver carbonate ($\mathrm{Ag_2CO_3}$) is strongly heated. Apply POAC on silver atoms to find the mass of the silver ($\mathrm{Ag}$) residue left behind. (Atomic masses: $\mathrm{Ag}=108$, $\mathrm{C}=12$, $\mathrm{O}=16$)
2.
A certain amount of iron ($\mathrm{Fe}$) was completely oxidized to form 160 g of ferric oxide ($\mathrm{Fe_2O_3}$). Using the Principle of Atom Conservation on iron, calculate the initial mass of iron taken. (Atomic mass: $\mathrm{Fe}=56$)
